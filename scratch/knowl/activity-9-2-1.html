Activity 1.104. Negative Exponents.

Consider the two lists below, and fill in the unknown values by following the pattern. Notice that as we move down the list, we can find each new entry by dividing the previous entry by the base.

Answer the following questions about your lists:

1.

  1. What did you find for the values of \(2^0 \) and \(5^0\)

  2. If you make a list with another base (say, \(3 \text{,}\) for example), what will you find for the value of \(3^0\text{?}\)

  3. Explain why this is true.

2.

  1. Compare the values of \(2^{3}\) and \(2^{-3}\text{.}\) Do you see a relationship between them?

  2. What about the values of \(5^2\) and \(5^{-2} \text{?}\) Try to state a general rule about powers with negative exponents.

  3. Use your rule to guess the value of \(3^{-4} \)

3.

Write each expression without exponents.

  1. \(-6^2 \)

  2. \(6^{-2} \)

  3. \({-6}^{-2} \)

4.

Write each expression without negative exponents.

  1. \(-4t^{-2} \)

  2. \((4t)^{-2} \)

  3. \(\left(\dfrac{x}{3} \right)^{-4} \)
