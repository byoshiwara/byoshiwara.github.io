Activity 1.80. Using the Quadratic Formula.

1.

  1. Use the quadratic formula to solve \(~ 2x^2=7-4x\)

    \(\begin{aligned}[t] \amp\blert{\text{Write the equation in standard form.}}\\ \\ \amp\blert{\text{Identify the coefficients.} \quad a=\underline{\qquad\qquad}, \quad b=\underline{\qquad\qquad}, \quad c=\underline{\qquad\qquad}.} \\ \\ \amp\blert{\text{Substitute the values of }a, ~b, ~\text{ and }~c~ \text{ into the quadratic formula:}}\\ \\ \amp\qquad\qquad\blert{x=\dfrac{-b\pm\sqrt{b^2-4ac}}{2a}=}\\ \\ \amp\blert{\text{Simplify the expression.}}\\ \end{aligned} \)

  2. Find decimal approximations to two decimal places for the solutions.

    \(\blert{\text{Use a calculator to approximate each solution.}} \)

2.

  1. Solve the equation \(~x^2-\dfrac{x}{3} = \dfrac{4}{3}~ \) by factoring.

    (Hint: Clear the fractions first!)

  2. Solve the same equation using the quadratic formula.
