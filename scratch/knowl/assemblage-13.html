Sign Patterns for Factoring Quadratic Trinomials.

Assume that \(b\text{,}\) \(c\text{,}\) \(p\text{,}\) and \(q\) are positive integers. Then

  1. \(x^2+bx+c=(x+p)(x+q) \)

    If all the coefficients of the trinomial are positive, then both \(p\) and \(q\) are positive.

  2. \(x^2-bx+c=(x-p)(x-q) \)

    If the linear term of the trinomial is negative and the other two terms positive, then both \(p\) and \(q\) are negative.

  3. \(x^2 \pm bx-c=(x+p)(x-q) \)

    If the constant term of the trinomial is negative, then \(p\) and \(q\) have opposite signs.
