Step 1

We look for a product of the form

\begin{equation*} x^2-12x+20=(x+p)(x+q) \end{equation*}

The numbers \(p\) and \(q\) must satisfy two conditions:

  1. Their product, \(pq\text{,}\) is the Last term, and so much equal 20.

  2. Their sum, \(p+q\text{,}\) must equal \(-12~\) (because \(\blert{O+I}=px+qx=-12x\text{.}\))

    These two conditions tell us that \(p\) and \(q\) must both be negative

Step 2

We list all the ways to factor 20 with negative factors

\begin{equation*} -1~ \text{ and } ~ -20, \qquad -2~ \text{ and } ~-10, \qquad ~ -4 \text{ and } ~-5 \end{equation*}
Step 3

We check each possibility to see which one gives the correct middle term:

\begin{align*} \amp(x-1)(x-20)\amp\amp O+I=-20x-x=-21x \\ \amp(x-2)(x-10)\amp\amp \blert{O+I=-10x-2x=-12x} \\ \amp(x-4)(x-5)\amp\amp O+I=-5x-4x=-9x \end{align*}

The second possibility is correct, so the factorization is

\begin{equation*} x^2-12x+20=(x-2)(x-10) \end{equation*}
