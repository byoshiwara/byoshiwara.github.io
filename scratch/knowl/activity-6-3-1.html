Activity 1.75. Factoring a Quadratic Trinomial.

\(\large{\blert{\text{Case 1 Positive coefficients:} ~x^2+bx+c}}\)

If all the coefficients in the quadratic trinomial are positive, then \(p\) and \(q\) are also positive.

Example 1.39. .

Factor \(t^2+9t+18\) as a product of two binomials

\begin{equation*} t^2+9t+18= \big( ~\underline{\hphantom{0000}} + \underline{\hphantom{0000}} ~\big) \big( ~\underline{\hphantom{0000}} + \underline{\hphantom{0000}} ~\big) \end{equation*}
Solution
1.

Factor each trinomial.

  1. \(x^2+8x+15\)

  2. \(y^2+14y+49\)

\(\large{\blert{\text{Case 2 Negative linear coefficients:} ~x^2-bx+c}}\)

If the coefficient of the linear term is negative and the constant term is positive, what are the signs of \(p\) and \(q\text{?}\)

Example 1.40. .

Factor \(x^2-12x+20 \) as a product of two binomials.

Solution
2.

Factor each trinomial.

  1. \(m^2-10m+24\)

  2. \(m^2-11m+24\)

\(\large{\blert{\text{Case 3 Negative constant term:} ~x^2+bx-c~ \text{ or } ~x^2-bx-c}}\)

The linear term can be either positive or negative

Example 1.41. .

Factor \(x^2+2x-15 \text{.}\)

Solution
3.

Factor each trinomial.

  1. \(t^2+8t-48 \)

  2. \(t^2-8t-48 \)
