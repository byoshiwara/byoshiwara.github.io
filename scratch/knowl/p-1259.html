  1. Graph the line of slope \(\dfrac{-1}{2} \) that passes through the point \((-3,2) \text{.}\)

    \(\blert{\text{Plot the point }(-3,2).} \)

    \(\blert{\text{Use the slope to find a second point.}} \)

    \begin{equation*} \blert{m=\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}=\dfrac{-1}{2} } \end{equation*}

    \(\blert{\text{Use the slope to find a third point.}} \)

    \begin{equation*} \blert{m=\frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}=\dfrac{1}{-2}} \end{equation*}

  2. Find an equation for the line.

    \(\begin{aligned} \amp\blert{\text{Step 1}}\amp\amp \blert{\text{Use the point-slope formula } ~\dfrac{y-y_1}{x-x_1}=m} \\ \\ \amp\blert{\text{Step 2}}\amp\amp \blert{\text{Cross-multiply to simplify the equation. }} \\ \\ \amp\blert{\text{Step 3}}\amp\amp \blert{\text{Solve for } y.} \\ \\ \end{aligned} \)
