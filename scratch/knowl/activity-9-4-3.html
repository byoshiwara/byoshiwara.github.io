Activity 1.112. Application.

In this Activity we use a formula for the surface area of a pyramid with a square base, as shown in the figure at right.

The pyramid has five faces, namely, the square base and four triangular sides. We know the height, \(h\text{,}\) of the pyramid, and the length,\(s\text{,}\) of the base.

To calculate thhe surface area of the pyramid, we add the area of its base and the areas of the four triangular faces.

\begin{align*} \blert{S}\amp\blert{= (\text{area of base})+4(\text{area of triangular face})} \\ \amp=s^2 + 4\cdot \frac{1}{4}s \sqrt{4h^2+s^2} \\ \amp= s^2 + s\sqrt{4h^2+s^2} \end{align*}

Use the formula to find the surface area of the Sun Pyramid in Mexico. The pyramid is 210 feet high and has a square base 689 feet on each side.
