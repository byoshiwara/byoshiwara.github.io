Step 1

We look for a product of the form

\begin{equation*} x^2+2x-15=(x+p)(x+q) \end{equation*}

Their product, \(pq\) of the two unknown numbers must be negative, \(-15\text{.}\) This means that \(p\) and \(q\) must have opposite signs, one positive and one negative. How do we decide which is positive and which negative? WE guess! If we make the wrong choice, we can easily fix it by switching the signs.

Step 2

There are only two ways to factor 15, either 1 times 15 or 3 times 5. We just guess that the second factor is negative, and check \(\blert{O+I} \) for each possibility:

\begin{align*} \amp(x+1)(x-15)\amp\amp \color{blue}{O+I=-15x+x=-14x }\\ \amp(x+3)(x-5)\amp\amp \color{blue}{O+I=-5x+3x=-\boldsymbol{2x}} \end{align*}
Step 3

The second possibility gives a middle term of \(-2x\text{.}\) This is not quite correct, because the middle term we want is \(2x\text{.}\) We fix this by switching the signs on the factors of \(-15\text{:}\) instead of using \(+3\) and \(-5\text{,}\) we change to \(-3\) and \(+5\text{.}\) You can check that the correct factorization is

\begin{equation*} x^2 +2x-15=(x-3)(x+5) \end{equation*}
