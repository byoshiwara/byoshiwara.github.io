Example 9.1. .

Solve \(4x-5=7\)

\begin{align*} \amp \blert{\text{Operations performed on}~x} \amp \amp \blert{\text{Steps for solution}}\\ \amp 1.~~~\text{Multiply by 4} \amp \amp 1.~~~\text{Add 5}\\ \amp 2.~~~\text{Subtract 5} \amp \amp 2.~~~\text{Divide by 4} \end{align*}
\begin{align*} 4x-5\amp =7\amp\amp\blert{\text{Add 5 to both sides.}} \\ 4x-5\blert{+5} \amp =7\blert{+5} \\ 4x \amp =12\amp\amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by }4.} \\ \frac{4x}{\blert{4}}\amp= \frac{12}{\blert{4}} \\ x\amp=3 \end{align*}

The solution is \(3\text{.}\) \(\qquad \blert{\text{Check:}}\quad 4(\alert{3})-5=7 \)

\begin{align*} \frac{-3t}{4}\amp =6\amp\amp\blert{\text{Multiply both sides by 4.}} \\ \blert{4}\left(\frac{-3t}{4} \right)\amp =(6)\blert{4} \\ -3t \amp =24\amp\amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by }-3.} \\ \frac{-3t}{\blert{-3}}\amp= \frac{24}{\blert{-3}} \\ t\amp=-8 \end{align*}

Activity 9.3. Inequalities.
1.

Properties of inequalities.

  1. Add a positive number, say 4, to both sides of the inequality, to get

    \begin{align*} 3 \blert{+4}\amp \lt 5+\blert{4} \\ 7 \amp\lt 9 \end{align*}

  2. Subtract the same quantity, say 6, from both sides, to get

    \begin{align*} 3 \blert{ -6}\amp \lt 5 \blert{ -6} \\ -3 \amp\lt -1 \end{align*}

  3. Multiply both sides of the inequality by a positive number, say 2, to get

    \begin{align*} \blert{ 2} (3) \amp \lt \blert{ 2} (5) \\ 6 \amp\lt 10 \end{align*}

  4. Divide both sides by a positive number, say 4, to get

    \begin{align*} \frac{3}{\blert{4}} \amp \lt \frac{5}{\blert{4}} \end{align*}

  5. Multiply both sides by a negative number, say \(-2\text{.}\)

    \begin{align*} \blert{ -2} (3) \amp \lt \blert{ -2} (5) \\ -6 \amp\lt -10 \amp\amp\blert{\text{False!}} \end{align*}
