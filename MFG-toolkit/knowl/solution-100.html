Solution 4.5.1.
  1. Compute \(2^3\) first, then subtract the result from \(-4\text{:}\)
    \begin{equation*} -4-2^3=-4-8=-12 \end{equation*}
  2. Compute \((-2)^3\) first, then multiply the result by \(-4\text{:}\)
    \begin{equation*} -4(-2)^3=-4(-8)=32 \end{equation*}
  3. Compute \((-4-2)\) first, then cube the result:
    \begin{equation*} (-4-2)^3=(-6)^3=-216 \end{equation*}
