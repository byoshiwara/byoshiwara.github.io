We assume that the number of cups is proportional to the amount of coffee beans. That is, the ratio of cups to coffee beans is constant. So

\begin{align*} \dfrac{225~ \text{cups}}{3~ \text{pounds}} \amp = \dfrac{3000~ \text{cups}}{x~ \text{pounds}}\\ \dfrac{225}{3} \amp = \dfrac{3000}{x} \end{align*}

Cross-multiplying, we find

\begin{align*} 225x \amp = 3(3000)\\ x \amp = \dfrac{3(3000)}{225}= 40 \end{align*}

You will need 40 pounds of coffee beans.
