Solution 2.28.1.
  1. We subtract 3 from the formula for \(f(x)\text{:}\) \(~~g(x)=\dfrac{1}{x}-3\)
  2. We add 4 to \(x\) before applying the function. Think of replacing \(x\) by \(x+4\) wherever it appears in the formula for \(f(x)\text{:}\) \(~~g(x)=\dfrac{1}{(x+4)^2}\)
  3. We multiply the formula for \(f(x)\) by \(\dfrac{1}{3}\text{:}\) \(~~g(x)=\dfrac{1}{3}\sqrt[3]{x}\)
  4. We make the formula for \(f(x)\) negative: \(~~g(x)=-|x|\)
