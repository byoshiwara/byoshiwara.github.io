The three properties of lagarithms are helpful in making computations involving logs.

Properties of Logarithms.

If \(x\text{,}\) \(y\text{,}\) \(b \gt 0\text{,}\) and \(b\ne 1\text{,}\) then

  1. \(\displaystyle \log_{b}{(xy)} = \log_{b}{x} + \log_{b}{y}\)

  2. \(\displaystyle \log_{b}\dfrac{x}{y} = \log_b x - \log_b y\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \log_b {x^k} = k \log_b x \)
