MathDL History

  • January 2000: National Science Foundation (NSF) issues solicitation for a NSDL (National Science Digitial Library, a.k.a., National STEM Digital Library, a.k.a. National STEM education Distributed Learning).
  • September 2000: The Mathematical Association of America (MAA) Mathematical Sciences Digital Library (MathDL) proposal is funded
  • January 2001: MathDL goes live (in time for the Joint Mathematics Meetings).
  • 2003-2004: MathDL hardware and software moves from Math Forum to MAA offices.
  • 2004 MAA's Math Gateway is one of four proposals funded in the first NSF solicitation for NSDL Pathways, intermediate library structures within NSDL.
  • 2008: MAA merges MathDL and Math Gateway under the MathDL name.
  • 2011: NSF announces it will no longer support the NSDL program. NSDL continues, but recent focus is more on K-12 education. MathDL has funding through September 2013, after which the MAA will have to assume support of MathDL.
