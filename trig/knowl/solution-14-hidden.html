We substitute \(x=\alert{-1}\) into the equation for the circle, and solve for \(y\text{.}\) \begin{align*} (\alert{-1}^2)+y^2 \amp = 4\\ y^2 \amp = 4-1 = 3\\ y \amp = \pm \sqrt{3}\\ \end{align*} The points are \((-1, \sqrt{3})\) and \((-1,\sqrt{3})\text{,}\) as shown at right.
