We apply the tangent identity to replace \(\tan \theta\) by \(\dfrac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta}\) and obtain

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \cos \theta \alert{\tan \theta} + \sin \theta \amp = \cos \theta (\alert{\dfrac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta}}) + \sin \theta\\ \amp = \sin \theta + \sin \theta = 2\sin \theta\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
