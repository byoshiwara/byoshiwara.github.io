Item 5.

To convert an equation from Cartesian to polar coordinates, we replace each \(x\) with \(r\cos (\theta)\) and each with \(y\) with \(r\sin)\text{.}\) To convert an equation from polar to Cartesian coordinates, look for expressions of the form \(r\cos (\theta),~r\sin (\theta),~r^2\text{,}\) or \(\tan (\theta)\text{.}\)
