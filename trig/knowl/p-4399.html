The order in which we apply transformations to a function makes a difference in the graph. We'll compare the graphs of the two functions

\begin{equation*} f(x) = \sin [2(x - \dfrac{\pi}{3})]~~~~ \text{and} ~~~~ g(x)= \sin (2x - \dfrac{\pi}{3}) \end{equation*}

Graph the two functions in the ZTrig window, along with \(y = \sin 2x\text{,}\) as shown below. Each graph involves a horizontal shift relative to \(y = \sin 2x\text{,}\) but the graph of \(f\) is shifted \(\dfrac{\pi}{3}\) units to the right, while the graph of \(g\) is shifted only \(\dfrac{\pi}{6}\) units to the right. This difference occurs because of the order of the transformations.
