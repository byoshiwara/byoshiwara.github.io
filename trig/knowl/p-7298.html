A complex number \(z=r(\cos \alpha + i\sin \alpha)\) in polar form has \(n\) complex \(n\)th roots, given by
\begin{equation*} \blert{z_k = r^{1/n}\left(\cos \left(\dfrac{\alpha + 2\pi k}{n}\right) + i\sin \left(\dfrac{\alpha + 2\pi k}{n}\right)\right)} \end{equation*}
for \(k = 0,~1,~2, \cdots,~ n-1\text{.}\)
