  1. Because \(\dfrac{5\pi}{2} = 2\pi + \dfrac{\pi}{2},~\) \(\theta\) is coterminal with \(\dfrac{\pi}{2},~\) and it has the same sine and cosine as \(\dfrac{\pi}{2}\text{.}\) And \(\dfrac{\pi}{2}\) radians is equal to \(90\degree\text{,}\) so
    \begin{gather*} \sin (\theta) = \sin \left(\dfrac{\pi}{2}\right) = 1\\ \cos (\theta) = \cos \left(\dfrac{\pi}{2}\right) = 0 \end{gather*}
