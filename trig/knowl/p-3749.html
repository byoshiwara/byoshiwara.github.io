At this point, you may feel that expressions such as \(~\cos \left(\dfrac{5\pi}{4}\right) = \dfrac{-1}{\sqrt{2}}~\) look like hieroglyphics. Keep in mind that \(\dfrac{5\pi}{4}\) and \(\dfrac{-1}{\sqrt{2}}\) are just numbers, so the equation above says that "the cosine of an angle of 3.9 radians is about -0.7."
