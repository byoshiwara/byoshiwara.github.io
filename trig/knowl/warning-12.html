Caution 3.28.

In the last example we used the value of \(\sin A\) to find angle \(A\text{.}\) But there are two angles that satisfy \(\sin A = 0.3723\text{,}\) one acute (\(21.9\degree\)) and one obtuse (\(168.1\degree\)). How did we know which one to choose?

In this case, the obtuse angle is too big to fit in the triangle, because angle \(B = 55\degree\text{.}\) Sometimes both angles will produce (different) triangles, and sometimes only the acute angle will work. You should always check whether both angles provide solutions.
