Exercise 25.

Martin is viewing a 4-meter tall painting whose base is 1 meter above his eye level.

  1. Write a formula for \(\alpha\text{,}\) the angle subtended from Martin's eye level to the bottom of the painting, when he stands \(x\) meters from the wall.
  2. Write a formula for \(\beta\text{,}\) the angle subtended by the painting, in terms of \(x\text{.}\)
  3. Evaluate the formula in part (b) for \(x=5\text{,}\) and interpret the result.
