Solution 8.48.1.

Because \(-\dfrac{\pi}{2} \le \theta \le 0\text{,}\) we draw a reference triangle in the fourth quadrant, as shown below.

Because \(\sec \theta = 3 = \dfrac{3}{1}\text{,}\) we label the horizontal leg with \(x=1\) and the hypotenuse with \(r=3\text{.}\) From the Pythagorean theorem, we find \(y=-\sqrt{8}=-2\sqrt{2}\text{.}\) We can now compute the values of the six trigonometric ratios.

\begin{align*} \cos \theta \amp =\dfrac{x}{r}=\dfrac{1}{3} \amp \amp \sec \theta =\dfrac{r}{x}=\dfrac{3}{1}=3\\ \sin \theta \amp =\dfrac{y}{r}=\dfrac{-2\sqrt{2}}{3} \amp \amp \csc \theta =\dfrac{r}{y}=\dfrac{3}{-2\sqrt{2}}=\dfrac{-3\sqrt{2}}{4}\\ \tan \theta \amp =\dfrac{y}{x}=\dfrac{-2\sqrt{2}}{1}=-2\sqrt{2} \amp \amp \cot \theta =\dfrac{x}{y}=\dfrac{1}{-2\sqrt{2}}=\dfrac{-\sqrt{2}}{4} \end{align*}
