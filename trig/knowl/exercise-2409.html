Exercise 83.

This problem outlines a geometric proof of difference of angles formula for tangent.

  1. In the figure below left, \(\alpha=\angle ABC\) and \(\beta = \angle DBC\text{.}\) Write expressions in terms of \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) for the sides \(AC,~DC,\) and \(AD\text{.}\)

  2. In the figure above right, explain why \(\triangle ABC\) is similar to \(\triangle FBE\text{.}\)
  3. Explain why \(\angle FDC = \alpha\text{.}\)
  4. Write an expression in terms of \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) for side \(CF\text{.}\)
  5. Explain why \(\triangle FBE\) is similar to \(\triangle ADE\text{.}\)
  6. Justify each equality in the statement
    \begin{equation*} \tan (\alpha - \beta) = \dfrac{DE}{BE} = \dfrac{AD}{BF} = \dfrac{\tan \alpha - \tan \beta}{1+\tan \alpha \tan \beta} \end{equation*}
