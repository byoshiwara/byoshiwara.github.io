For example, \(\sec (0.8)\) is not equal to \(\cos^{-1}(0.8)\text{.}\) Remember that \(~\alert{\cos^{-1}(0.8)}~\) is an angle, namely the angle whose cosine is 0.8, while \(~\alert{\sec (0.8)}~\) is the reciprocal of the cosine of 0.8 radians, or \(\dfrac{1}{\cos (0.8)}\text{.}\) You can check on your calculator that
\begin{equation*} \cos^{-1}(0.8)=0.6435~\text{radians},~~~~\text{and}~~~~\sec (0.8)=1.4353 \end{equation*}
