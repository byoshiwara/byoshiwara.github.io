Solution2.6.1

We make a sketch of the situation, as shown at right, and label any known dimensions. We'll call the unknown height \(h\text{.}\) The ladder forms the hypotenuse of a right triangle, so we can apply the Pythagorean theorem,substituting 25 for \(c\text{,}\) 7 for \(b\text{,}\) and \(h\) for \(a\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} a^2 + b^2 \amp = c^2\\ h^2 + 7^2 \amp = 25^2\\ \end{align*} Now solve by extraction of roots: \begin{align*} h^2 + 49 \amp = 625 \amp\amp \text{Subtract 49 from both sides.}\\ h^2 \amp = 576 \amp\amp \text{Extract roots.}\\ h \amp = \pm \sqrt{576} \amp\amp \text{Simplify the radical.}\\ h \amp = \pm 24 \end{align*} The height must be a positive number, so the solution \(-24\) does not make sense for this problem. The ladder reaches 24 feet up the wall.
