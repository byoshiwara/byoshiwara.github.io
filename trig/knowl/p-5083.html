We evaluate either expression for \(t\) to find the other coordinate of the intersection point.

\begin{equation*} \text{when}~~~~\theta = \dfrac{\pi}{4},~~~~r = 2 + 2\sin \dfrac{\pi}{4} = 2 + (\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}}) = 2 + \sqrt{2} \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \text{when}~~~~\theta = \dfrac{5\pi}{4},~~~~r = 2 + 2\sin \dfrac{5\pi}{4} = 2 + (\dfrac{-1}{\sqrt{2}}) = 2 - \sqrt{2} \end{equation*}
