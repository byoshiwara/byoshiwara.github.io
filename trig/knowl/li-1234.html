Item B.

We can move a vertex around the circle without changing the measure of its angle.

  1. Measure angle \(C\) in your triangle. We say that this angle subtends the arc joining \(A\) and \(B\) on the circle.

  2. Choose any other point on the circle that is not on the arc \(\overset{\large\frown}{AB}\text{.}\) Call this point \(D\text{.}\) Draw the line segments \(\overline{AD}\) and \(\overline{BD}\) to create a second triangle. Measure the angle at \(D\text{;}\) it should be the same as the angle \(C\text{.}\)

  3. In fact, all angles with vertex on the circle that subtend the same arc have the same measure. Verify this fact by creating two more angles that subtend the arc \(\overset{\large\frown}{AB}\text{.}\)
