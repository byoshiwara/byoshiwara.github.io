\begin{equation*} \cos (2) = -0.4161~~~~\text{and} ~~~~ \sin (2) = 0.9093 \end{equation*}
so the coordinates of point \(P\) on the unit circle in the figure at right are \((-0.4161, 0.9093)\text{.}\) Therefore,
\begin{equation*} \tan (2) = \dfrac{0.9093}{-0.4161} = -2.1853 \end{equation*}
