Solution 2.23.1.

  1. Enter TAN 58 to find \(\tan 58\degree = 1.6003\text{,}\) rounded to four decimal places.

  2. We use the tangent ratio to write an equation. In this triangle, the angle is \(58 \degree\text{.}\)

    \begin{equation*} \tan 58\degree = \dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} \end{equation*}

    Next we fill in the value of \(\tan 58\degree\text{,}\) and the lengths of the sides.

    \begin{equation*} 1.6003 = \frac{h}{300} \end{equation*}

    Solving for \(h\) gives

    \begin{equation*} h = 300(1,6003) = 480.0 \end{equation*}

    so the height of the cliff is about 480 feet.
