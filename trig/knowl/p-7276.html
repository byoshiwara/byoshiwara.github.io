In general, we can write the real and imaginary parts of \(z\) in terms of \(r\) and \(\theta\) as
\begin{equation*} a=r \cos (\theta)~~~~\text{and}~~~~b=r\sin (\theta) \end{equation*}
Thus, the complex number \(z=a+bi\) can also be written as
\begin{equation*} z=(r\cos(\theta))+(r\sin (\theta))i~~~~\text{or}~~~~z=r(\cos(\theta)+i\sin (\theta))\text{.} \end{equation*}
