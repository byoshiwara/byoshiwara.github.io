  1. Distance Formula.
    The distance \(d\) between two points \(P_{1}(x_1, y_1)\) and \(P_{2}(x_2, y_2)\) is
    \begin{equation*} d=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2+(y_2-y_1)^2} \end{equation*}
  2. Any number that can be written as a quotient of two integers \(~~\dfrac{a}{b},~~\text{where}~~ b\not=0,\) is called a rational number. The decimal form of a rational number is either a terminating decimal or a repeating decimal.
  3. An irrational number is one that cannot be written as a quotient of two integers \(~~\dfrac{a}{b},~~\text{where}~~ b\not=0\text{.}\) We cannot write down an exact decimal equivalent for an irrational number.
  4. A circle is the set of all points in a plane that lie at a given distance, called the radius, from a fixed point called the center.
  5. Circle.
    The equation for a circle of radius \(r\) centered at the origin is
    \begin{equation*} x^2+y^2=r^2 \end{equation*}
  6. The circle \(~x^2+y^2=1~\text{,}\) which is centered at the origin and has radius 1 unit, is called the unit circle.
  7. Circumference of a Circle.
    The circumference of a circle of radius \(r\) is given by
    \begin{equation*} C=2\pi r \end{equation*}
  8. Area of a Circle.
    The area of a circle of radius \(r\) is given by
    \begin{equation*} A=\pi r^2 \end{equation*}
