Exercise 33.
In the right triangle \(ABC\) shown, \(\angle A = 30\degree,~\angle C = 90\degree\text{,}\) and \(c = 3\) inches.
  1. Use the definition of \(\sin (A)\) to solve for \(a\) (the length of side \(\overline{BC}\)).
  2. Can you draw a triangle \(ABC\) with \(A = 30\degree\) and \(c = 3\) if \(a \lt \dfrac{3}{2}\text{?}\) Why or why not?
  3. How many triangles are possible if \(\dfrac{3}{2} \lt a \lt 3\text{?}\)
  4. How many triangles are possible if \(a \gt 3\text{?}\)
