  1. We subtract multiples of \(360\degree\) from \(520\degree\) until the remainder is less than \(360\degree\text{.}\)
    \begin{equation*} 520\degree - 360\degree = 160\degree \end{equation*}
    Because \(160\degree\) is between \(0\degree\) and \(360\degree\text{,}\) this is the angle we want.
  2. You can use your calculator to see that
    \begin{align*} \sin (160\degree) \amp = \sin (520\degree) = 0.3420\\ \cos (160\degree) \amp = \cos (520\degree) = -0.9397\\ \tan (160\degree) \amp = \tan (520\degree) = -0.3640 \end{align*}
