Item a.

First, we write the input for the cosine function in factored form:

\begin{equation*} f(x) = 4\cos 3\left(x+\dfrac{\pi}{3}\right) - 2 \end{equation*}

Comparing the formula for \(f\) with the standard form, we see that \(A=4\text{,}\) \(B=3,~h = \dfrac{-\pi}{3}\text{,}\) and \(k = 2\text{.}\) Thus, the midline is \(y = -2\text{,}\) the amplitude is \(4\text{,}\) the period is \(\dfrac{2\pi}{3}\text{,}\) and the horizontal shift is \(\dfrac{\pi}{3}\) units to the left.
