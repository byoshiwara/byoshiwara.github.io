Think of \(\cos (t)\) as a single variable, and multiply by each term inside parentheses. (The algebraic form of the calculation is shown on the right in blue).
\begin{align*} \cos (t) \Big(3 \cos (t) - 2\Big)\amp = \Big(\cos (t)\Big)\Big(3 \cos (t)\Big) - \Big(\cos (t)\Big)\cdot 2 \amp \amp \blert{x (3x-2) = x\cdot 3x - x\cdot 2}\\ \amp = 3\cos^2 (t) - 2\cos (t) \amp \amp \blert{=3x^2 - 2x} \end{align*}
Notice that we write \(\Big(\cos (t)\Big)^2\) as \(\cos^2 (t)\text{.}\)
