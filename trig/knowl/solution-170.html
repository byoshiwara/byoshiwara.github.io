Solution 9.6.1

The vector \(\frac{2}{3}\bf{v}\) points in the same direction as \(\bf{v}\text{,}\) but is only two-thirds as long as the vector \(\bf{v}\text{.}\) Because \(\bf{v}\) has a slope of \(\dfrac{-2}{5}\text{,}\) so does any nonzero multiple of \(\bf{v}\text{.}\) The vector \(-\sqrt{3}\bf{v}\) points in the direction opposite to \(\bf{v}\) and is \(\sqrt{3} \approx 1.7\) times as long as \(\bf{v}\text{.}\) Both vectors are shown in figure (b).
