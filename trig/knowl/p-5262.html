The letter \(i\) used in this way is not a variable, it is the name of a specific number, and hence is a constant. The square root of any negative number can be written as the product of a real number and \(i\). For example, \begin{align*} \sqrt{-4} \amp = \sqrt{-1 \cdot 4}\\ \amp = \sqrt{-1} \sqrt{4} = i \cdot 2\\ \end{align*} or \(\sqrt{-4} = 2\text{.}\) Any number that is the product of \(i\) and a real number is called an imaginary number.
