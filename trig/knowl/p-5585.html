We take the reciprocal of each side of the equation to obtain
\begin{equation*} \sin (\theta) = \dfrac{3}{2\sqrt{3}} = \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \end{equation*}
Because \(\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) is one of the special values, we recognize that one of the solutions is \(\theta =\dfrac{\pi}{3}\text{.}\) The sine and the cosecant are also positive in the second quadrant, so the second solution is \(\pi - \dfrac{\pi}{3} = \dfrac{2\pi}{3}\text{.}\)
