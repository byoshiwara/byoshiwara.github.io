We replace \(~\sec \theta~\) by \(~\dfrac{1}{\cos \theta}~\) and \(~\tan \theta~\) by \(~\dfrac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta}~\) to get

\begin{align*} \dfrac{1}{\cos \theta} - \dfrac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta} \cdot \sin \theta \amp = \dfrac{1}{\cos \theta} - \dfrac{\sin^2 \theta}{\cos \theta}\\ \amp = \dfrac{1 - \sin^2 \theta}{\cos \theta} \\ \amp= \dfrac{\cos^2 \theta}{\cos \theta} \\ \amp= \cos \theta \end{align*}
