For our example, we start with the formula for the cubing function: \begin{align*} y \amp = x^3 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Interchange \(x\) and \(y\text{.}\)}}\\ x \amp = y^3 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Solve for \(y\text{.}\)}}\\ y \amp = \sqrt[3]{x} \amp\amp \blert{\text{A formula for the inverse function.}}\\ \end{align*} We use the notation \(\blert{f^{-1}(x)}\) to denote the inverse function. Thus, we have just shown that the inverse function for \(f(x)=x^3\) is \(f^{-1}(x)=\sqrt[3]{x}\text{.}\)
