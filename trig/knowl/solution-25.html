Solution 2.23.1

First, we use the Pythagorean theorem to find the hypotenuse, \(c\text{.}\) \begin{align*} c^2 \amp =6^2 + 8^2\\ c^2 \amp = 536=84 = 100 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Take square roots.}}\\ c \amp = \sqrt{100} = 10\\ \end{align*} For the angle \(\theta\text{,}\) the opposite side is 8 inches long, and the adjacent side is 6 inches long, as shown in the figure. Thus, \begin{align*} \sin \theta \amp = \dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \frac{8}{10}~~ \text{or}~~ 0.8\\ \cos \theta \amp = \dfrac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}} = \frac{6}{10} ~~ \text{or}~~ 0.6\\ \tan \theta \amp = \dfrac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}} = \frac{8}{6} ~~ \text{or}~~1.\overline{3}\\ \end{align*}
