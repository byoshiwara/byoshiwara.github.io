If we know the polar coordinates , we can compute the Cartesian coordinates and as the legs of a right triangle.

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{x}{r} = \cos \theta, ~~ \text{so} ~~ x = r\cos \theta \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \dfrac{y}{r} = \sin \theta, ~~ \text{so} ~~ y = r\sin \theta \end{equation*}
