\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] a^2 + b^2 \amp = c^2\\ h^2 + 7^2 \amp = 25^2\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Now solve by extraction of roots:

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] h^2 + 49 \amp = 625 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Subtract 49 from both sides.}}\\ h^2 \amp = 576 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Extract roots.}}\\ h \amp = \pm \sqrt{576} \amp\amp \blert{\text{Simplify the radical.}}\\ h \amp = \pm 24 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

The height must be a positive number, so the solution \(-24\) does not make sense for this problem. The ladder reaches 24 feet up the wall.
