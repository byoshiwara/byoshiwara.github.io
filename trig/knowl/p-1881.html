  1. In standard position, an angle of \(200\degree\) lies in the third quadrant. Its reference angle is
    \begin{equation*} 200\degree - 180\degree = 20\degree \end{equation*}
  2. Both angles are shown at right. Note that the reference triangle for \(200\degree\) is congruent to the reference triangle for \(20\degree\text{.}\) You can use your calculator to verify the following values.
    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \sin 20\degree \amp = 0.3420 \amp\amp \sin 200\degree = -0.3420\\ \cos 20\degree\amp = 0.9397 \amp\amp \cos 200\degree = -0.9397\\ \tan 20\degree \amp = 0.3640 \amp\amp \tan 200\degree = 0.3640\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
