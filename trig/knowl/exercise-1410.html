Exercise 57.

Recall that to calculate a fraction of a revolution in degrees we divide the angle by \(360\degree\text{.}\) For example, \(90\degree\) is \(\dfrac{1}{4}\) of a revolution because \(\dfrac{90\degree}{360\degree} = \dfrac{1}{4}\text{.}\)
  1. Write an expression that gives the fraction of a revolution for an angle \(\theta\) in radians.
  2. Use your expression to calculate what fraction of a revolution is represented by each of the following angles: \(~\theta = \dfrac{3\pi}{4}, ~~ \theta = \dfrac{5\pi}{3}, ~~ \theta = \dfrac{7\pi}{6}\text{.}\)
