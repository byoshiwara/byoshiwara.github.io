We observe that \(75\degree = 45\degree + 30\degree\text{,}\) so \(\tan 75\degree = \tan (45\degree + 30\degree)\text{.}\) We can apply the sum formula for tangent.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \tan(45\degree + 30\degree) \amp = \dfrac{\tan 45\degree + \tan 30\degree}{1 - \tan 45\degree \tan 30\degree}\\ \amp = \dfrac{1+\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{3}}}{1-1(\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{3}})} \cdot \blert{\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{\sqrt{3}}} =\dfrac{\sqrt{3}+1}{\sqrt{3}-1} \\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
