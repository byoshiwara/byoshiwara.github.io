Remember that \(\cos \dfrac{\pi}{4} = \dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2},~~\cos\dfrac{\pi}{6} =\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2},~~\sin\dfrac{\pi}{4} = \dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\text{,}\) and \(\sin \dfrac{\pi}{6} = \dfrac{1}{2}\text{.}\) Substituting all these values into the difference formula for cosine, we obtain the following.
\begin{align*} \cos\dfrac{\pi}{12} \amp = \cos \left(\dfrac{\pi}{4} - \dfrac{\pi}{6}\right)\\ \amp = \cos \dfrac{\pi}{4} \cos \dfrac{\pi}{6} + \sin \dfrac{\pi}{4} \sin \dfrac{\pi}{6}\\ \amp = \dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \cdot \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} +\dfrac{\sqrt{2}}{2} \cdot \dfrac{1}{2}= \dfrac{\sqrt{6}+\sqrt{2}}{4} \end{align*}
You can check that your calculator gives the same decimal approximation of about 0.9659 for both \(\cos \dfrac{\pi}{12}\) and \(\dfrac{\sqrt{6}+\sqrt{2}}{4}\text{.}\)
