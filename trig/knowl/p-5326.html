  1. Keep in mind that \(\arccos \left(\dfrac{1}{3}\right)\) is an angle. We'll call it \(\theta\text{,}\) so that \(\theta = \arccos \left(\dfrac{1}{3}\right)\text{,}\) and hence \(\cos (\theta) = \dfrac{1}{3}\text{.}\) Then substituting \(\theta\) for \(\arccos \left(\dfrac{1}{3}\right)\) in the original expression gives us
    \begin{equation*} \cos\left[\left(\arccos \dfrac{1}{3}\right)\right] = \cos (\theta) = \dfrac{1}{3} \end{equation*}

We start inside the parentheses: \(\sin \left(\dfrac{3\pi}{2}\right) = -1\text{.}\) So \(\arcsin\left[\sin \left(\dfrac{3\pi}{2}\right)\right] = \arcsin(-1)\text{.}\) Now \(\arcsin(-1)\) is the angle between \(\dfrac{-\pi}{2}\) and 0 whose sine is \(-1\text{,}\) and that angle is \(\dfrac{-\pi}{2}\text{.}\) Thus, \(\arcsin\left[\sin \left(\dfrac{3\pi}{2}\right)\right] = \dfrac{-\pi}{2}\)
