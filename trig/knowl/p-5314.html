Francine's house lies under the flight path from the city airport, and commercial airliners pass overhead at an altitude of 15,000 feet. As Francine watches an airplane recede, its angle of elevation, \(\theta\text{,}\) decreases.

  1. Write a formula for the horizontal distance, \(d\text{,}\) to the airplane as a function of \(\theta\text{.}\)
  2. Write a formula for \(\theta\) as a function of\(d\text{.}\)
  3. Evaluate the formula in part (b) for \(d=20,000\text{,}\) and interpret the result.
