  1. On a unit circle, the coordinates of \(P\) are \((\cos \dfrac{\pi}{2}, \sin \dfrac{\pi}{2})\text{,}\) so

    \begin{equation*} x = \cos \dfrac{\pi}{2} = 0~~~\text{and}~~~ y = \sin \dfrac{\pi}{2} = 1 \end{equation*}

    The coordinates of \(P\) are \((0,1)\text{,}\) as shown at right.

    (We could also observe that an arc of length \(\dfrac{\pi}{2}\) is one quarter of a unit circle, so the point \(P\) sits at the twelve o'clock position on the circle.)

  2. The coordinates of \(P\) are \((\cos \dfrac{5\pi}{6}, \sin \dfrac{5\pi}{6})\text{,}\) so

    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] x \amp= \cos \dfrac{5\pi}{6} = \dfrac{-\sqrt{3}}{2}~~~\\ y \amp= \sin \dfrac{5\pi}{6} = \dfrac{1}{2}\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

    Thus, the coordinates of \(P\) are \((\dfrac{-\sqrt{3}}{2},\dfrac{1}{2})\text{,}\) as shown at right.
