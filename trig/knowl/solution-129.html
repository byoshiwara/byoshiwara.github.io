Solution 7.20.1.

The midline of the graph is \(y=0\text{,}\) and its amplitude is \(4\text{.}\) It has the shape of a cosine, but its maximum value occurs at \(x=\dfrac{\pi}{6}\) instead of at \(x=0\text{;}\) so the graph is shifted \(\dfrac{\pi}{6}\) units to the right, compared to the graph of \(y=\cos x\text{.}\)

The graph completes one cycle between \(x=\dfrac{\pi}{6}\) and \(x=\dfrac{13\pi}{6}\text{,}\) so its period is \(2\pi\text{.}\) Thus, we use the standard form \(y = A\cos B(x-h) + k\) with \(A=4,~B=1,~h=\dfrac{\pi}{6}\text{,}\) and \(k=0\text{,}\) giving us

\begin{equation*} y = 4\cos (x-\dfrac{\pi}{6}) \end{equation*}

as our formula for the function.
