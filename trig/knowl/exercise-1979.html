Exercise 91.

Follow the steps to prove the difference of angles formula for cosine,

\begin{equation*} \cos(\alpha - \beta) = \cos\alpha \cos\beta + \sin\alpha \sin\beta \end{equation*}

  1. Write an expression for \((AB)^2\text{,}\) the square of the distance between the points \(A\) and \(B\text{,}\) using the Law of Cosines for \(\triangle AOB\text{.}\)

  2. Write another expression for \((AB)^2\) using the distance formula and the coordinates of \(A\) and \(B\text{.}\)

  3. Equate the two expressions for \((AB)^2\) you obtained in (a) and (b). Simplify the equation to obtain \(\cos(\alpha - \beta) = \cos\alpha \cos\beta + \sin\alpha \sin\beta\text{.}\)
