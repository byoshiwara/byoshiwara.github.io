Recall that \(\sin \dfrac{2\pi}{3} = \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\) and \(\cos \dfrac{2\pi}{3} = \dfrac{-1}{2}\text{.}\) Substituting these values into the sum formula for sine, we find
\begin{align*} \sin\left(\theta + \dfrac{2\pi}{3}\right) \amp = \sin \theta \cos \dfrac{2\pi}{3} +\cos \theta \sin \dfrac{2\pi}{3}\\ \amp = 0.6 \left(\dfrac{-1}{2}\right) +(-0.8)\left(\dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2}\right)\\ \amp = \dfrac{3}{5} \cdot \dfrac{-1}{2} + \dfrac{-4}{5} \cdot \dfrac{\sqrt{3}}{2} = \dfrac{-3-4\sqrt{3}}{10} \end{align*}
