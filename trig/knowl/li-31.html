Item D.2.

The Pythagorean theorem states that:

\begin{align*} \amp\text{IF}:\quad a,~b,~ \text{and}~c~ \text{are the sides of a right triangle, and}~c~ \text{is the hypotenuse,}\\ \amp\text{THEN}:~~a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \end{align*}

The "if" part of the theorem is called the hypothesis, and the "then" part is called the conclusion. The converse of a theorem is the new statement you obtain when you interchange the hypothesis and the conclusion. Write the converse of the Pythagorean theorem.
