Activity 8.5. Reciprocal Ratios.

Part 1

The figure shows a unit circle centered at \(O\text{.}\) The line through \(AC\) is tangent to the circle at \(A\text{.}\) (Recall that a tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius that meets it.)

  1. What is the radius of the circle?

  2. Find line segments whose lengths are \(\cos \theta\) and \(\sin \theta\text{.}\)

  3. Explain why \(\triangle OAC\) is similar to \(\triangle OBD\text{.}\)

  4. Find a line segment in \(\triangle OAC\) whose length is \(\tan \theta\text{.}\) Explain why your choice is correct.

  5. Find a line segment in \(\triangle OAC\) whose length is \(\sec \theta\text{.}\) Explain why your choice is correct.

  6. Find a line segment in \(\triangle OAC\) whose length is \(\csc \theta\text{.}\) Explain why your choice is correct.

Part 2

Here is another unit circle, with tangent \(BD\text{.}\)

  1. What is the measure of the angles \(\angle OCD\) and \(\angle OCB\text{?}\)

  2. Explain why \(\triangle OCB\) is similar to \(\triangle OAC\text{.}\)

  3. Find a line segment in \(\triangle OCB\) whose length is \(\tan \theta\text{.}\) Explain why your choice is correct.

  4. Find a line segment in \(\triangle OCB\) whose length is \(\sec \theta\text{.}\) Explain why your choice is correct.

  5. Explain why \(\triangle OCB\) is similar to \(\triangle DCO\text{.}\)

  6. Find a line segment in \(\triangle DCO\) whose length is \(\csc \theta\text{.}\) Explain why your choice is correct.
