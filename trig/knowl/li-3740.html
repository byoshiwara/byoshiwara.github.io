Item b.
Because \(\cos \dfrac{7\pi}{4}\) is positive, the angle \(\cos^{-1}\left(\cos \dfrac{7\pi}{4}\right)\) is a first quadrant angle whose cosine is the same as \(\cos \dfrac{7\pi}{4}\text{.}\) This angle is \(\dfrac{\pi}{4}\text{,}\) so \(\cos^{-1}\left(\cos \dfrac{7\pi}{4}\right)=\dfrac{\pi}{4}\text{.}\)
