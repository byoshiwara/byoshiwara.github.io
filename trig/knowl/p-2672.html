Think of \(\cos t\) as a single variable, and multiply by each term inside parentheses. (The algebraic form of the calculation is shown on the right in blue).

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \cos t (3 \cos t - 2)\amp = (\cos t)(3 \cos t) - (\cos t)\cdot 2 \amp \amp \blert{x (3x-2) = x\cdot 3x - x\cdot 2}\\ \amp = 3\cos^2 t - 2\cos t \amp \amp \blert{=3x^2 - 2x}\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Notice that we write \((\cos t)^2\) as \(\cos^2 t\text{.}\)
