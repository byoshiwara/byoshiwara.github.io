Caution 10.48

You are used to plotting ordered pairs \((x,y)\) in the Cartesian plane, so that one point represents two real numbers, given by its coordinates. However, in the complex plane one point represents a single complex number \(z=a+bi\text{,}\) and the coordinates of the point are the real and imaginary parts of \(z\text{.}\) For example, the point \((2,-6)\) in the real plane represents the pair of real numbers \(x=2\) and \(t=-6\text{,}\) but the point \((2,-6)\) in the complex plane represents the complex number \(z=2-6i\text{.}\)
