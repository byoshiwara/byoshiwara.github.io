Depending on its phase, the moon looks like a disk that is partially visible and partially in shadow. The visible fraction ranges from 0% to 100%, and can be approximated by a sinusoidal function \(V(t)\text{,}\) where \(t\) is the number of days since the last full moon. The time between successive full moons (a lunar month) is 29.5 days.
