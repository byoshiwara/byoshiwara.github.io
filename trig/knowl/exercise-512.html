Exercise 40.

  1. Sketch the line \(y = \dfrac{5}{3}x\text{.}\)

  2. Find two points on the line with positive \(x\)-coordinates.

  3. The line \(y = \dfrac{5}{3}x\) makes an angle with the positive \(x\)-axis. What is that angle?

  4. Repeat parts (a) through (c) for the line \(y = \dfrac{-5}{3}x\text{,}\) except find two points with negative \(x\)-coordinates.
