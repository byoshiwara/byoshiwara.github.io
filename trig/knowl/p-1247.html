Now we apply the Law of Sines, using angles \(B\) and \(C\text{.}\) \begin{align*} \dfrac{b}{\sin B} \amp = \dfrac{c}{\sin C} \amp\amp \blert{\text{Substitute the given values.}}\\ \dfrac{d}{\sin 79.4\degree} \amp = \dfrac{400}{\sin 17.4\degree} \amp\amp \blert{\text{Evaluate sines.}}\\ \dfrac{d}{0.9829} \amp = \dfrac{400}{0.2990} \amp\amp \blert{\text{Multiply both sides by 0.9829.}} \\ d \amp = 1315 \\ \end{align*} The ship is about 1315 yards from the observer at \(A\text{.}\)
