Item 4
Conversion Equations
  1. To convert from polar coordinates \((r, \theta)\) to Cartesian: \begin{align*} x \amp = r\cos \theta\\ y \amp = r \sin \theta\\ \end{align*}
  2. To convert from Cartesian coordinates \((x,y)\) to polar: \begin{align*} \amp r = \sqrt{x^2+y^2}\\ \amp \tan \theta = \dfrac{y}{x}\\ \end{align*}

where the choice of \(\theta\) depends on the quadrant.
