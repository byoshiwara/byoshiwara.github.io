The half-life of hydrogen-3 is 12.5 years.

  1. If a sample initially contains \(N_0\) grams of hydrogen-3, how much will it contain after 12.5 years? How much will it contain after 25 years?

  2. Use your answers to part (a) to sketch a graph of \(N(t)\text{,}\) the amount of hydrogen-3 remaining, versus time. (Choose an arbitrary height for \(N_0\) on the vertical axis.)

  3. Calculate \(k\text{,}\) and hence find a decay law of the form \(N(t) = N_0e^{kt}\text{,}\) where \(k\lt 0\text{,}\) for hydrogen-3.
