Item b.
Both asymptotes pass through the center of the hyperbola, \((3,-2)\text{.}\) Their slopes are
\begin{gather*} \frac{\sqrt{10}}{\sqrt{8}} = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{2}\quad \text{ and } \dfrac{-\sqrt{5}}{2} \end{gather*}
We substitute these values into the point-slope formula to find the equations
\begin{gather*} y + 2 = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{2}(x-3)\quad \text{ and } \quad y + 2 = \frac{-\sqrt{5}}{2}(x-3) \end{gather*}
