We cannot cancel the 4's in Example 3a, because 4 is not a factor of the entire numerator. Thus,

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{4x+2}{4} \ne x+2 \end{equation*}

In Example 3b, we cannot cancel the 3's, because they are terms and not factors. Thus,

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{9x^2+3}{6x+3} \ne \dfrac{9x^2}{6x} \end{equation*}
