1. We choose the points \((2,400)\) and \((4, 700)\text{,}\) as shown on the graph. Then

    \begin{gather*} m=\frac{\Delta a}{\Delta t} = \frac{300 \text{ feet}}{2 \text{ minutes}} = 150 \text{ feet/minute} \end{gather*}

  2. The slope gives the rate of change of altitude with respect to time. The skier rises at a rate of 150 feet per minute.

  3. A linear model has the form

    \begin{gather*} y = (\text{starting value}) + (\text{rate}) \times t \end{gather*}

    From the graph, we see that at \(t = 0\) the skier's altitude was \(a = 100\text{,}\) and we calculated the rate of change of altitude as 150 feet per minute. Substituting these values into the formula, we find

    \begin{gather*} a = 100 + 150t \end{gather*}
