Item a.
To find the half-life, we calculate the time elapsed when only half the original amount, or 100 mg, is left.
\begin{align*} 200(0.73)^t \amp = 100\amp\amp \glert{\text{Divide both sides by 200.}}\\ 0.73^t \amp = 0.5\amp\amp \glert{\text{Take the log of both sides.}}\\ t\log 0.73 \amp = \log 0.5\amp\amp \glert{\text{Divide both sides by log 0.73.}}\\ t \amp= \frac{\log 0.5}{\log 0.73} = 2.2 \end{align*}
The half-life is 2.2> hours.
