Solution 1.84.1.

  1. We check to see if the slope between each pair of points is the same.

    \begin{align*} m \amp = \dfrac{T_2-T_1}{S_2-S_1} = \dfrac{31.328-31.552}{12-8} = -0.056\\ m \amp = \dfrac{T_3-T_2}{S_3-S_2} = \dfrac{30.88 - 31.328}{20-12} = -0.056 \end{align*}

    Because the slopes are the same, the data could describe a linear model.

  2. Step 1: In part (a) we found the slope: \(m = -0.056\)

    Step 2: We substitute the slope and either point into the point-slope formula.

    \begin{align*} T-T_1 \amp = m(S-S_1)\\ T-30.88 \amp = -0.056(S-20)\\ T-30.88 \amp = -0.056S + 1.12\\ T \amp = -0.056S + 32 \end{align*}
  3. If \(T=32\) in our equation, then \(S\) must be zero, so pure water (no salinity) freezes at 32\(\degree\)F.
  4. We substitute 35 for \(S\) in our equation.
    \begin{equation*} T = -0.056(35)+32 = 30.04 \end{equation*}
    Sea water freezes at 30.04\(\degree\)F.
