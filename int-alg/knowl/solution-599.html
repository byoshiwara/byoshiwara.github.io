Solution 6.84.1.

We simplify each cube root by factoring perfect cubes from the radicals.

\begin{align*} \sqrt[3]{40x^2} \amp-3\sqrt[3]{16x^2}+\sqrt[3]{54x^2} = \\ \amp = \sqrt[3]{\alert{8} \cdot 5x^2}-3\sqrt[3]{\alert{8} \cdot 2x^2}+\sqrt[3]{\alert{27} \cdot 2x^2} \amp \amp \glert{\text{Extract roots.}}\\ \amp = \alert{2}\sqrt[3]{5x^2}-3 \cdot \alert{2}\sqrt[3]{2x^2}+\alert{3}\sqrt[3]{2x^2}\\ \amp = 2\sqrt[3]{5x^2}-6\sqrt[3]{2x^2}+3\sqrt[3]{2x^2} \amp \amp \glert{\text{Combine like radicals.}}\\ \amp = 2\sqrt[3]{5x^2}-3\sqrt[3]{2x^2} \end{align*}
