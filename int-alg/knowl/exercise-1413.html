Exercise 34.

Many insects grow by discrete amounts each time they shed their exoskeletons. Dyar’s rule says that the size of the insect increases by a constant ratio at each stage. (Source: Burton, 1998.)

  1. Dyar measured the width of the head of a caterpillar of a swallowtail butterfly at each stage. The caterpillar’s head was initially approximately 42 millimeters wide and was 63.84 millimeters wide after its first stage. Find the growth ratio.

  2. Write an equation for the width of the caterpillar’s head at the \(n\)th stage.

  3. Graph your equation on your calculator.

  4. What head width does the model predict after 5 stages?
