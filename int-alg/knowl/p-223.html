The \(d\)-intercept tells us that when \(d = 240\text{,}\) \(g = 0\text{,}\) or that when Leon has traveled 240 miles, he has 0 gallons of gasoline left; the fuel tank is empty. The \(g\)-intercept tells us that when \(d = 0\text{,}\) \(g = 20\text{,}\) or that when Leon has traveled 0 miles, he has 20 gallons of gasoline left. The fuel tank holds 20 gallons when full.
