What is the difference between the slope formula
\begin{gather*} m= \frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1} \end{gather*}
and the point-slope formula
\begin{gather*} m= \frac{y-y_1}{x-x_1} ? \end{gather*}
They are really the same formula, but they are used for different purposes:
  1. The slope formula is used to calculate the slope when we know two points. We know \((x_1, y_1)\) and \((x_2, y_2)\text{,}\) and we are looking for \(m\text{.}\)
  2. The point-slope formula is used to find the equation of a line. We know \((x_1, y_1)\) and \(m\text{,}\) and we are looking for \(y = mx + b\text{.}\)
