Item a.

One-half of \(-12\) is \(-6\text{,}\) so the constant term is \((-6)^2\text{,}\) or \(36\text{.}\) We add \(36\) to obtain

\begin{align*} x^2 - 12x \alert{{}+{}36}=(x - 6)^2\amp\amp\amp  {\large\glert{\underset{p^2\, = \,(-6)^2 \,=\, 36\hphantom{0}}{\stackrel {p\, = \,\frac{1}{2}(-12) \,=\, -6}{}}}} \end{align*}
