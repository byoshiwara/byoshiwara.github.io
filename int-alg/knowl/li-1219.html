Because \(x\) is squared in this equation, we perform the opposite operation, or take square roots, in order to solve for \(x\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} x^2 \amp = 9 \amp \amp \glert{\text{Take square roots of both sides.}}\\ x \amp = \pm \sqrt{9} = \pm 3 \amp \amp \glert{\text{Remember that every positive number has two square roots.}} \end{align*}
