This complex fraction contains the simple fractions \(\dfrac{1}{2x},~ \dfrac{1}{x^2},~ \dfrac{1}{4}\) and \(\dfrac{1}{2x}\text{.}\) The LCD of these fractions is \(4x^2\text{.}\) We multiply the numerator and denominator of the complex fraction by \(4x^2\text{.}\) Doing this will not change the value of the fraction, but will clear all the "smaller" fractions inside.

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{\dfrac{1}{2x} - \dfrac{1}{x^2}}{\dfrac{1}{4} - \dfrac{1}{2x}} =\dfrac{\alert{4x^2}(\dfrac{1}{2x} - \dfrac{1}{x^2})}{\alert{4x^2}(\dfrac{1}{4} - \dfrac{1}{2x})} \end{equation*}

Then we apply the distributive law, so that we multiply each term of the numerator and each term of the denominator by \(\alert{4x^2}\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{\alert{4x^2}(\dfrac{1}{2x} - \dfrac{1}{x^2})}{\alert{4x^2}(\dfrac{1}{4} - \dfrac{1}{2x})} = \dfrac{\alert{\dfrac{4x^2}{1}} \cdot \dfrac{1}{2x} - \alert{\dfrac{4x^2}{1}} \cdot \dfrac{1}{x^2}}{\alert{\dfrac{4x^2}{1}} \cdot \dfrac{1}{4} - \alert{\dfrac{4x^2}{1}} \cdot \dfrac{1}{2x}}=\dfrac{2x-4}{x^2-2x} \end{equation*}

Finally, we reduce the result to obtain

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{2x-4}{x^2-2x} = \dfrac{2\cancel{(x-2)}}{x\cancel{(x-2)}} = \dfrac{2}{x} \end{equation*}
