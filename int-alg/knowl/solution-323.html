Solution 9.4.4.1.

We first divide each side by 16 to obtain
\begin{equation*} \frac{y^2}{4} - \dfrac{x^2}{16} = 1\quad\text{ or } \quad \frac{y^2}{2^2} - \dfrac{x^2}{4^2} = 1 \end{equation*}
The graph is a central hyperbola with \(y\)-intercepts \(2\) and \(-2\text{,}\) as shown in the figure. The slopes of the asymptotes are given by
\begin{equation*} \pm \frac{b}{a}=\pm \frac{2}{4}=\pm \frac{1}{2} \end{equation*}
so the equations of the asymptotes are
\begin{equation*} y= \dfrac{1}{2} x\quad \text{ and } y= \frac{-1}{2} x \end{equation*}
