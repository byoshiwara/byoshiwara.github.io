For this equation, \(a=\alert{2},~b=\alert{-1},~\) and \(c=\alert{2}\text{.}\) We substitute these values into the quadratic formula to obtain

\begin{equation*} x=\dfrac{-(\alert{-1})} \pm \sqrt{(\alert{-1})^2-4(\alert{2})(\alert{2})}}{2(\alert{2})} = \dfrac{1 \pm \sqrt{-15}}{4} \end{equation*}

We write the solutions as

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{1 \pm i\sqrt{15}}{4}~~~\text{or}~~~\dfrac{1}{4} \pm \dfrac{\sqrt{15}}{4} \end{equation*}
