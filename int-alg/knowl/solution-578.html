Solution 9.9.1.

First we find the slope of the desired line, then use the point-slope formula to write its equation. The line we want is perpendicular to the given line, so its slope is the negative reciprocal of \(m_1 = 2\text{,}\) the slope of the given line. Thus

\begin{gather*} m_2=\frac{-1}{m_1}=\frac{-1}{2} \end{gather*}

Now we use the point-slope formula with \(m =\dfrac{-1}{2}\) and \((x_1, y_1) = (1, 4)\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} y-y_1 \amp=m(x-x_1) \\ y-4 \amp=\frac{-1}{2}(x-1) \amp\amp\blert{\text{Apply the distributive law.}}\\ y-4 \amp=\frac{-1}{2}x+\frac{1}{2} \amp\amp\blert{\text{Add 4 to both sides.}}\\ y \amp= \frac{-1}{2}x+\frac{9}{2} \amp\amp\blert{\frac{1}{2}+4=\frac{1}{2}+\frac{8}{2}=\frac{9}{2} } \end{align*}
