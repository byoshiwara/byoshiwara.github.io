An algebraic fraction (or rational expression, as they are someitmes called,) is a fraction in which both numerator and denominator are fractions. Here are some examples of algebraic fractions:

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{3}{x},~~~~~~\dfrac{a^2+1}{a-2},~~~~~~\text{and}~~~~~~\dfrac{z-1}{2z+3} \end{equation*}

We can evaluate algebraic fractions just as we evaluate any other algebraic expression.
