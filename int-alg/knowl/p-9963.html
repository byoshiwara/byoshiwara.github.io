We are not going to plot the logs from the table, we are going to plot powers of 10 that give the actual masses of the animals. For example, the mass of the horse is 300 kg, and we saw that \(~\log {(300)} = 2.48~\text{,}\) or \(~10^{2.48} = 300.~\) So to plot the mass of the horse, we plot \(10^{2.48}\text{.}\) The powers of 10 on our number line are evenly spaced, so we plot \(10^{2.48}\) about halfway between \(10^2\) and \(10^3\text{.}\)
