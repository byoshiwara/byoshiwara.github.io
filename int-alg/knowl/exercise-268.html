Exercise 19.
According to the Bureau of the Census, the average age of the U.S. population is steadily rising. The table gives data for two types of average, the mean and the median, for the ages of women. (The "mean" is the familiar average, and the "median" is the middle age: half of all women are older and half younger than the median.)
Date 1990 1992 1994 1996 1998
Median age \(34.0\) \(34.6\) \(35.2\) \(35.8\) \(36.4\)
Mean age \(36.6\) \(36.8\) \(37.0\) \(37.3\) \(37.5\)
  1. Which is growing more rapidly, the mean age or the median age?
  2. Plot the data for median age versus the date, using 1990 as \(t=0\text{.}\) Draw a line through the data points.
  3. What is the meaning of the slope of the line in part (b)?
  4. Plot the data for mean age versus date on the same grid. Draw a line that fits the data.
  5. Use your graph to estimate when the mean age and the median age were the same.
  6. If the median age of women in the United States is greater than the mean age, what does this tell you about the population of women?
