To multiply a fraction by a whole number, we write the whole number with 1 as denominator.

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{2}{3} \cdot \alert{4} = \dfrac{2}{3} \cdot \alert{\dfrac{4}{1}} = \dfrac{8}{3} \end{equation*}

The same applies to the product of an algebraic fraction and any nonfractional expression. For example,

\begin{equation*} \alert{6x}\left(\dfrac{2}{x^2-x}\right) = \alert{\dfrac{6x}{1}} \cdot \dfrac{2}{x^2-x} = \dfrac{6\cancel{x}}{1} \cdot \dfrac{2}{\cancel{x}(x-1)} = \dfrac{12}{x-1} \end{equation*}
