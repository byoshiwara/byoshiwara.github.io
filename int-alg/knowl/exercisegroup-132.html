Exercise Group 11–14.

For Problems 11–14, use the graph.

11.

  1. Find \(h(-3), ~h(1),\) and \(h(3)\text{.}\)

  2. For what value(s) of \(z\) is \(h(z)=3\text{?}\)

  3. Find the intercepts of the graph. List the function values given by the intercepts.

  4. What is the maximum value of \(h(z)\text{?}\)

  5. For what value(s) of \(z\) does \(h\) take on its maximum value?

  6. On what intervals is the function increasing? Decreasing?

12.

  1. Find \(R(1)\) and \(R(3)\text{.}\)

  2. For what value(s) of \(p\) is \(R(p)=2\text{?}\)

  3. Find the intercepts of the graph. List the function values given by the intercepts.

  4. Find the maximum and minimum values of \(R(p)\text{.}\)

  5. For what value(s) of \(p\) does \(R\) take on its maximum and minimum values?

  6. On what intervals is the function increasing? Decreasing?

13.

  1. Find \(S(0), ~S\left(\dfrac{1}{6}\right),\) and \(S(-1)\text{.}\)

  2. Estimate the value of \(S\left(\dfrac{1}{3}\right)\) from the graph.

  3. For what value(s) of \(x\) is \(S(x)=\dfrac{-1}{2}\text{?}\)

  4. Find the maximum and minimum values of \(S(x)\text{.}\)

  5. For what value(s) of \(x\) does \(S\) take on its maximum and minimum values?

  6. On what intervals is the function increasing? Decreasing?

14.

  1. Find \(F(-3),~F(-2)\) and \(F(2)\text{.}\)

  2. For what value(s) of \(s\) is \(F(s)=-1\text{?}\)

  3. Find the maximum and minimum values of \(F(s)\text{.}\)

  4. For what value(s) of \(s\) does \(F\) take on its maximum and minimum values?
