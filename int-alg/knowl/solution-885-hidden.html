1. We write each power as a fraction, then simplify.

    \begin{align*} x^{-1}-y^{-1} \amp = \dfrac{1}{x} - \dfrac{1}{y} \amp \amp \glert{\text{The LCD is}~xy.}\\ \amp = \dfrac{1}{x} \cdot \dfrac{y}{y}- \dfrac{1}{y} \cdot \dfrac{x}{x}\\ \amp = \dfrac{y-x}{xy} \end{align*}

  2. We simplify the expression inside parentheses first.

    \begin{align*} (x^{-2}+y^{-2})^{-1} \amp = (\dfrac{1}{x^2} + \dfrac{1}{y^2})^{-1} \amp \amp \glert{\text{Add fractions.}}\\ \amp = (\dfrac{y^2+x^2}{x^2y^2})^{-1} = \dfrac{x^2y^2}{x^2+y^2} \end{align*}

    For the last step, remember that a negative exponent indicates the reciprocal of its base.
