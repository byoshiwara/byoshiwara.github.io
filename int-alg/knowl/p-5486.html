Consider the power \(x^{3/2}\text{.}\) Notice that the exponent \(\dfrac{3}{2}= 3\left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)\text{,}\) and thus by the third law of exponents, we can write
\begin{equation*} \left(x^{1/2}\right)^3 = x^{(1/2)^3} = x^{3/2} \end{equation*}
In other words, we can compute \(x^{3/2}\) by first taking the square root of \(x\) and then cubing the result. For example,
\begin{align*} 100^{3/2} \amp = \left(\alert{100^{1/2}}\right)^3 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Take the square root of 100.}}\\ \amp= \alert{10^3} = 1000 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Cube the result.}} \end{align*}
