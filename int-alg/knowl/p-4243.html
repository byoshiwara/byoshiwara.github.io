The volume of a sphere varies directly with the cube of its radius. A balloon of radius 5 centimeters has volume \(\dfrac{500\pi}{3}\) cubic centimeters, or about 524 cubic centimeters.
  1. Find a formula for the volume, \(V\text{,}\) of a sphere as a function of its radius, \(r\text{.}\)
  2. What is the radius of a balloon whose volume is \(\dfrac{1000\pi}{3}\) cubic centimeters, or about 1028 cubic centimeters?
  3. Sketch a graph of the function.
