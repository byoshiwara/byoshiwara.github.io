If Andre's feet are at the origin, then the vertex of the ball's trajectory is the point \((\alert{10},\alert{11})\text{,}\) and its \(y\)-intercept is \((0,7)\text{.}\) We start with the vertex form for a parabola.

\begin{align*} y \amp =a(x-x_v)^2+y_v\\ y \amp =a(x-\alert{10})^2+\alert{11} \end{align*}

We use the point \((0,7)\) to find the value of \(a\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} \alert{7} \amp = a(\alert{0}-10)^2+11\\ 7 \amp= 100a+11\\ a \amp = -0.04 \end{align*}

The equation of the trajectory is \(~y=-0.04(x-10)^2+11\text{.}\)
