  1. If Andre's feet are at the origin, then the vertex of the ball's trajectory is the point \((\alert{10},\alert{11})\text{,}\) and its \(y\)-intercept is \((0,7)\text{.}\) We start with the vertex form for a parabola.

    \begin{align*} y \amp =a(x-x_v)^2+y_v\\ y \amp =a(x-\alert{10})^2+\alert{11} \end{align*}

    We use the point \((0,7)\) to find the value of \(a\text{.}\)

    \begin{align*} \alert{7} \amp = a(\alert{0}-10)^2+11\\ 7 \amp= 100a+11\\ a \amp = -0.04 \end{align*}

    The equation of the trajectory is \(~y=-0.04(x-10)^2+11\text{.}\)

  2. We'd like to know if the point \((15,10)\) is on the trajectory of Andre's free-throw. We substitute \(x=\alert{15}\) into the equation.

    \begin{align*} y \amp =-0.04(\alert{15}-10)^2+11\\ y \amp =-0.04(25)+11 = 10 \end{align*}

    From our computations, we see that the point \((15,10)\) is indeed on the trajectory. However, because Andre's shot will probably hit the backboard just where the hoop attaches and bounce off, so it is unlikely that his shot will score.
