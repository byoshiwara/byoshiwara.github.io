Solution 9.50.1.

  1. \(\dfrac{x^2}{8}-\dfrac{y^2}{2}=1 \text{.}\) A hyperbola centered at the origin with vertices \(\left(2\sqrt{2},0 \right)\) and \(\left(-2\sqrt{2},0 \right) \text{,}\) and asymptotes \(y = \dfrac{1}{2}x\) and \(y = -\dfrac{1}{2} x\text{.}\)

  2. \(y=-4x^2\text{.}\) A parabola opening downward with vertex at the origin.
