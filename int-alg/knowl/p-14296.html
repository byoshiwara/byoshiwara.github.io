We can also solve algebraically for the break-even points. The intersection points of the two graphs correspond to the solutions of the system of equations

\begin{align*} y\amp =0.15x^2 + 0.75x + 180\\ y \amp= 15x \end{align*}

We equate the two expressions for \(y\) and solve for \(x\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} 0.15x^2 + 0.75x + 180 \amp= 15x\amp\amp \glert{\text{Subtract }15x \text{ from both sides.}}\\ 0.15x^2 - 14.25x + 180 \amp= 0\amp\amp \glert{\text{Use the quadratic formula.}} \end{align*}
\begin{align*} x \amp= \frac{14.25 \pm\sqrt{14.25^2 - 4(0.15)(180)}}{2(-0.05)}\amp\amp \glert{\text{Simplify.}}\\ \amp= \frac{14.25 \pm 9.75}{0.3} \end{align*}

The solutions are 15 and 80, as we found from the graph.
