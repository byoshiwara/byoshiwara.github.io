In part (a) of the Example above, we can also use the laws of exponents to write the formula as
\begin{equation*} P(t)=10 \cdot (2^{1/5})^t \end{equation*}
From this form see that the growth factor for this function is \(2^{1/5}\text{,}\) or about 1.149. The flea population grows at about 14.9% per day.
