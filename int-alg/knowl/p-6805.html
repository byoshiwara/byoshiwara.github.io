We first divide each side by 16 to obtain

\begin{gather*} \frac{y^2}{4} - \dfrac{x^2}{16} = 1\quad\text{ or } \quad \frac{y^2}{2^2} - \dfrac{x^2}{4^2} = 1 \end{gather*}

The graph is a central hyperbola with \(y\)-intercepts \(2\) and \(-2\text{,}\) as shown in the figure. The slopes of the asymptotes are given by

\begin{gather*} \pm \frac{b}{a}=\pm \frac{2}{4}=\pm \frac{1}{2} \end{gather*}

so the equations of the asymptotes are

\begin{gather*} y= \dfrac{1}{2} x\quad \text{ and } y= \frac{-1}{2} x \end{gather*}
