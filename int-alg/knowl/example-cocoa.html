Example 2.4.

An outdoor snack bar collected the following data showing the number of cups of cocoa, \(C\text{,}\) they sold when the high temperature for the day was \(T\degree\) Celsius.

Temperature (\(\degree\text{C}\)), \(T\) 2 4 5 8 10 11 12 15 16 18
Cups of cocoa, \(C\) 45 42 42 35 25 25 17 16 15 6

  1. Make a scatterplot of the data, and draw a regression line

  2. Read values from your line for the number of cups of cocoa that will be sold when the temperature is \(8\degree\text{C}\) and when the temperature is \(16\degree\text{C}\text{.}\)

  3. Find an equation for the regression line.

  4. Use your equation to predict the number of cups of cocoa that will be sold when the temperature is \(9\degree\text{C}\text{,}\) and when the temperature is \(24\degree\text{C}.\)
