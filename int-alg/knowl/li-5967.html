Item b.
We write each factor as a power of \(3\text{.}\)
\begin{align*} 3^3 \cdot 3^{-2x} \amp= \left(3^2\right)^{x+1}\amp\amp\blert{27=3^3~\text{and}~9=3^2.} \end{align*}
We use the laws of exponents to simplify each side:
\begin{equation*} 3^{3-2x} = 3^{2x+2} \end{equation*}
Now we equate the exponents to obtain
\begin{align*} 3 - 2x \amp = 2x + 2\\ -4x =\amp -1 \end{align*}
The solution is \(x = \dfrac{1}{4}\text{.}\)
