Caution 9.13.

We cannot simplify \(\sqrt{4+16} \) as \(\sqrt{4}+\sqrt{16} \text{.}\) Remember that \(\sqrt{a^2+b^2}\ne a+b \text{.}\) You can easily see this by observing that

\begin{gather*} \sqrt{3^2+4^2}=\sqrt{9+16}=\sqrt{5} \end{gather*}

so it cannot be true that \(\sqrt{3^2+4^2} \) equals \(3+4\text{,}\) or 7. For the same reason, we cannot simplify the distance formula to \((x-x_1)+(y-y_1)\text{.}\)
