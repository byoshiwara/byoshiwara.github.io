Recall that the \(x\)-intercept of a line is the point where \(y=0\text{,}\) or where the line crosses the \(x\)-axis. We find the \(x\)-intercept by setting \(y=0\) in the equation of the line, and solving for \(x\text{.}\) We can find the \(x\)-intercepts of a parabola the same way.
