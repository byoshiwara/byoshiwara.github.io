True or false.

  1. When we raise a power to a power, we multiply the exponents; in symbols

    \begin{gather*} (a^m)^n = a^{mn} \end{gather*}

  2. \(\dfrac{4}{3} = 4\left(\dfrac{1}{3}\right)\text{,}\) and in general \(\dfrac{p}{q} = p\left(\dfrac{1}{q}\right)\text{.}\)

    • To compute \((25^{1/2})^3\text{,}\) we first take the square root of 25, then cube the result.

      • The notation \(16^{3/4}\) means to multipy 16 by \(\dfrac{3}{4}\text{.}\)
