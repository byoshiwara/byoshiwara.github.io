Solution 4.3.1.

Write the equation in standard form as

\begin{equation*} 2x^2 - 4x + 1 = 0 \end{equation*}

We substitute \(\alert{2}\) for \(a\text{,}\) \(\alert{-4}\) for \(b\text{,}\) and \(\alert{1}\) for \(c\) into the quadratic formula, then simplify.

\begin{align*} x \amp =\frac{-(\alert{-4})\pm \sqrt{(\alert{-4})^2 - 4(\alert{2})(\alert{1})}}{2(\alert{2})}\\ \amp = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{8}}{4} \end{align*}

Using a calculator, we find that the solutions are approximately \(1.7\) and \(0.3\text{.}\) These values are the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph of \(~y = 2x^2 - 4x + 1,~\) as shown in the figure.
