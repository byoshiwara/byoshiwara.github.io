Exercise 25.
The figure shows a graph of \(y =\dfrac{-x}{3} - 6\text{.}\)
  1. Use the graph to find all values of \(x\) for which
    1. \(\displaystyle y=-4\)
    2. \(\displaystyle y\gt -4\)
    3. \(\displaystyle y\lt -4\)
  2. Use the graph to solve
    1. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{-x}{3} - 6=-4\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{-x}{3} - 6\gt -4\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{-x}{3} - 6\lt -4\)
  3. Explain why your answers to parts (a) and (b) are the same.
