Solution A.5.48.1.
  1. \([3,6).\) This is called a half-open or half-closed interval. \(3\) is included in the interval, but \(6\) is not included.
  2. \([-9, \infty).\) We use round brackets next to the symbol \(\infty\) because \(\infty\) is not a specific number and is not included in the set.
  3. \((-\infty,1] \cup (4, \infty).\) The word “or” describes the union of two sets. The symbol \(~\cup~\) is used for union.
  4. \(\displaystyle (-8,-5] \cup [-1,3).\)
