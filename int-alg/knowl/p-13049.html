Now let's compute the slopes of the two lines in Example 1. Choose two points on the graph of \(y=-2\text{,}\) say \((-5, -2)\) and \((4, -2)\text{.}\) Use these points to compute the slope.

\begin{gather*} m=\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}= \frac{-2-(-2)}{4-(-5)}=\frac{0}{9}=0 \end{gather*}

The slope of the horizontal line \(y = -2\) is zero. In fact, the slope of any horizontal line is zero, because the \(y\)-coordinates of all the points on the line are equal. Thus

\begin{gather*} m=\frac{y_2-y_1}{x_2-x_1}=\frac{0}{x_2-x_1}=0 \end{gather*}
