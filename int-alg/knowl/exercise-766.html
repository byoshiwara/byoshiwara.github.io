Exercise 26.

Max took a sequence of photographs of an explosion spaced at equal time intervals. From the photographs he was able to estimate the height and vertical velocity of some debris from the explosion, as shown in the table. (Negative velocities indicate that the debris is falling back to earth.)

Velocity (meters/second) \(67\) \(47\) \(27\) \(8\) \(-12\) \(-31\)
Height (meters) \(8\) \(122\) \(196\) \(232\) \(228\) \(185\)

  1. Enter the data into your calculator and create a scatterplot. Fit a quadratic regression equation to the data, and graph the equation on the scatterplot.

  2. Use your regression equation to find the vertex of the parabola. What do the coordinates represent, in terms of the problem? What should the velocity of the debris be at its maximum height?
