1. The degree of \(P\) is 4, and the degree of \(Q\) is 3, so the degree of their product is \(4 + 3 = 7\text{.}\) The only degree 7 term of the product is \((5x^4)(3x^3) = 15x^7\text{,}\) which has coefficient 15.
  2. In the product, each term of \(P(x)\) is multiplied by each term of \(Q(x)\text{.}\) How might we isolate the products that result in terms sof degree 3? We get degree 3 terms by multiplying together terms of degree 0 and 3, or of degree 1 and 2. For these polynomials, the possible combinations are:
    \(P(x)\) \(Q(x)\) Product
    \(2\) \(3x^3\) \(6x^3\)
    \(-2x^3\) \(3\) \(-6x^3\)
    \(-x\) \(-4x^2\) \(4x^3\)
    \(6x^2\) \(5x\) \(30x^3\)
    The sum of the third-degree terms of the product is
    \begin{equation*} 6x^3-6x^3+4x^3+30x^3 = 34x^3\text{,} \end{equation*}
    with coefficient 34.
