To find the height that gives a BMI of \(25\text{,}\) we solve the equation \(25 = 319h^{-2}\text{.}\) Note that the variable \(h\) appears in the denominator of a fraction, so we begin by clearing the denominatorâ€”in this case we multiply both sides of the equation by \(h^2\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} 25 \amp = \frac{319}{h^2} \amp\amp \glert{\text{Multiply both sides by }h^2.}\\ 25h^2 \amp = 319 \amp\amp \glert{\text{Divide both sides by }25.}\\ h^2 \amp = 12.76 \amp\amp \glert{\text{Extract square roots.}}\\ h \amp\approx 3.57 \end{align*}

To have a BMI under \(25\text{,}\) Yarbrough would have to be over \(3.57\) meters, or \(11\) feet \(8\) inches tall. (In fact, he is \(6\) feet \(8\) inches tall.)
