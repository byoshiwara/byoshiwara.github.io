Which of the following are polynomials? If it is not a polynomial, explain why not.

  1. \(\displaystyle 5x^4-3x^2\)
  2. \(\displaystyle 3x+1+\dfrac{2}{x^2}\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{1}{2a^2+5a-6}\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{2}{3}t^2+\dfrac{1}{4}t^3+\dfrac{5}{8}\)
in-context =======

If a common factor occurs in a numerator and a denominator of either fraction, we can divide it out either before or after multiplying. For example,

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{3a}{4} \cdot \dfrac{5}{6a^2} = \dfrac{\cancel{3} \cdot \cancel{a}}{4} \cdot \dfrac{5}{\cancel{3} \cdot 2 \cdot \cancel{a} \cdot a} = \dfrac{5}{8a} \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{3a}{4} \cdot \dfrac{5}{6a^2} = \dfrac{15a}{24a^2} = \dfrac{\cancel{3} \cdot 5 \cdot\cancel{a}}{\cancel{3} \cdot 8 \cdot \cancel{a} \cdot a} = \dfrac{5}{8a} \end{equation*}

It is usually easier to cancel any common factors before multiplying.
