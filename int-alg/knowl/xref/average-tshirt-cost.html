Checkpoint 8.2.21. Practice 7.

EarthCare decides to sell T-shirts to raise money. The company makes an initial investment of $100 to pay for the design of the T-shirt and to set up the printing process. After that, the T-shirts cost $5 each for labor and materials.
  1. Express the average cost, \(C\text{,}\) per T-shirt as a function of the number of T-shirts EarthCare produces.
  2. Make a table of values for the function.
    \(x\) \(1\) \(2\) \(4\) \(5\) \(10\) \(20\)
    \(C\) \(\hphantom{000}\) \(\hphantom{000}\) \(\hphantom{000}\) \(\hphantom{000}\) \(\hphantom{000}\) \(\hphantom{000}\)
  3. Graph the function and explain what it tells you about the cost of the T-shirts.
Answer.
  1. \(\displaystyle C=g(x) = \dfrac{100+5x}{x}\)
  2. \(x\) \(1\) \(2\) \(4\) \(5\) \(10\) \(20\)
    \(C\) \(105\) \(55\) \(30\) \(25\) \(15\) \(10\)
  3. As the number of T-shirts increases, the average cost per shirt decreases, but eventually levels off and approaches $5 per T-shirt.
