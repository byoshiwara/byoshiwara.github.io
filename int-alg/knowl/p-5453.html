  1. Because \(v\) varies directly with \(t\text{,}\) there is a positive constant \(k\) for which

    \begin{equation*} v = kt\text{.} \end{equation*}

    We substitute \(v = \alert{39.2}\) and \(t = \alert{4}\) and solve for \(k\) to find

    \begin{align*} 39.2 \amp = k(4)\amp\amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by 4.}}\\ k \amp = 9.8 \end{align*}

    Replacing \(k\) by \(9.8\text{,}\) we find the formula \(v (t) = 9.8 t\text{.}\)

  2. We evaluate the function found in part (a) for \(t = 6\text{.}\)

    \begin{equation*} v = 9.8(\alert{6}) = 58.8 \end{equation*}

    At \(t = 6\) seconds, the rock is falling at a speed of 58.8 meters per second.
