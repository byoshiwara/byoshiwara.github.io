The distance, \(d\text{,}\) in miles that a person can see on a clear day from a height, \(h\text{,}\) in feet is given by

\begin{equation*} d = G(h) = 1.22\sqrt{h} \end{equation*}

  1. Evaluate \(G(20,320)\) and explain what it means.

  2. As \(h\) increases, does \(d\) increase or decrease? Support your answer with calculations.

  3. Estimate the height you need in order to see 100 miles. Write your answer with function notation.
