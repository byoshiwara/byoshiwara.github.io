One-half of \(5\) is \(\dfrac{5}{2}\text{,}\) so the constant term is \(\left(\dfrac{5}{2}\right)^2\text{,}\) or \(\dfrac{25}{4}\text{.}\) We add \(\dfrac{25}{4}\) to obtain

\begin{align*} x^2 +5x \alert{{}+{}\frac{25}{4}}=\left(x +\frac{5}{2}\right)^2 \amp\amp\amp {\large \glert{\underset{p^2\, = \,(\frac{5}{2})^2 \,= \,\frac{25}{4}}{\stackrel {p \,= \,\frac{1}{2}(5) \,=\, \frac{5}{2}~~ }{}}}} \end{align*}
