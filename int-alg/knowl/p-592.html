We can read the \(x\)-coordinates by dropping straight down to the \(x\)-axis, as shown by the arows. For example, the \(x\)-value corresponding to \(285-15x=150\) is \(x=9\text{.}\) For larger values of \(285-15x\text{,}\) we must choose \(x\)-values less than 9. Thus the solutions are all values of \(x\) less than or equal to 9, as shown on the \(x\)-axis.
