As we have seen in ehe examples above, the solutions of the quadratic equation

\begin{equation*} a(x - r_1)(x - r_2) = 0 \end{equation*}

are \(r_1\) and \(r_2\text{.}\) This is called the factored form of the quadratic equation. Thus, we know the two solutions of a quadratic equation, we can work backwards to reconstruct the equation.
