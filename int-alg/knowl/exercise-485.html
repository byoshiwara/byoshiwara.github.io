Exercise 33.

A box is made from a square piece of cardboard by cutting 2-inch squares from each corner and then turning up the edges.

  1. If the piece of cardboard is \(x\) inches square, write expressions for the length, width, and height of the box. Then write an expression for the volume, \(V\text{,}\) of the box in terms of \(x\text{.}\)
  2. Use your calculator to make a table of values showing the volumes of boxes made from cardboard squares of side 4 inches, 5 inches, and so on.
  3. Graph your expression for the volume on your calculator. What is the smallest value of \(x\) that makes sense for this problems
  4. Use your table or your graph to find what size cardboard you need to make a box with volume 50 cubic inches.
  5. Write and solve a quadratic equation to answer part (d).
