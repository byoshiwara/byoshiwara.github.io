In the previous example, do not try to simplify

\begin{equation*} 80 \cdot 6^{0.03x} \rightarrow 480^{0.03x}~~ \blert{\text{ Incorrect!}} \end{equation*}

Remember that the order of operations tells us to compute the power \(6^{0.03x}\) before multiplying by \(80\text{.}\) To solve the equation, we must first isolate the power with the variable exponent.
