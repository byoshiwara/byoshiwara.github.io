Item a.

The graph shown is called a scatterplot. The data are not strictly linear, because the slope is not constant: from 1960 to 1965, the minimum wage increased at an average rate of \(\dfrac{1.25-1.00}{5}=0.05\) dollars per year, and from 1970 to 1975, the minimum wage increased at a rate of \(\dfrac{2.10-1.60}{5}=0.10\) dollars per year. However, the data points do appear to lie close to an imaginary line.
