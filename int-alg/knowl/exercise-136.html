Exercise 22.
A traditional first experiment for chemistry students is to make 98 observations about a burning candle. Delbert records the height, \(h\text{,}\) of the candle in inches at various times \(t\) minutes after he lit it.
\(t\) \(0\) \(10\) \(30\) \(45\)
\(h\) \(12\) \(11.5\) \(10.5\) \(9.75\)
  1. Choose appropriate scales for the axes and plot the data. Do the points lie on a straight line?
  2. Compute the slope of the graph, including units, and explain what the slope tells us about the candle.
  3. Write a linear model for \(h\) in terms of \(t\text{.}\)
