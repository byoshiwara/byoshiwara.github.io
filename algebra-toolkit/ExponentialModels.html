Subsection 1. Solve equations algebraically

Subsubsection Examples

Example 4.54.

Solve \(~3x^{1.05} = 18~\text{.}\) Round your answer to hundredths.

Solution

This is a power equation. We divide both sides by 3 to isolate the variable, then raise both sides to the reciprocal of the exponent.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] (x^{1.05})^{1/1.05} \amp = 6^{1/1.05}\\ x \amp = 5.51 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Example 4.55.

Solve \(~3(1.05)^x = 18~\text{.}\) Round your answer to hundredths.

Solution

This is an exponential equation. We divide both sides by 3, then take logarithms.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \log(1.05^x) \amp = \log 6 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Apply the third log property.}}\\ x \log 1.05\amp = \log 6\\ x \amp \dfrac{\log 6}{\log 1.05} = 36.72 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Example 4.56.

Solve \(~9x^{3/5} = 36~\text{.}\) Round your answer to hundredths.

Solution

This is a power equation. We divide both sides by 9 to isolate the variable, then raise both sides to the reciprocal of the exponent.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] (x^{3/5}^{5/3} \amp = 4^{5/3}\\ x \amp = 10.08 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Example 4.57.

Solve \(~1.5(3^{x/5}) = 12~\text{.}\) Round your answer to hundredths.

Solution

This is an exponential equation. We divide both sides by 1.5, then take logarithms.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \log 3^{x/5} \amp = \log 8 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Apply the third log property.}}\\ \dfrac{x}{5} \log 3 \amp = \log 8\\ x \amp \dfrac{5\log 8}{\log 3} = 9.46 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Subsection 2. Calculate gowth and decay rates

Subsubsection Examples

Example 4.62.

The half-life of a cold medication in the body is 6 hours. Find its decay rate.

Solution

The decay law for the medication is \(N=N_0(0.5)^{t/8}\text{.}\) We can rewrite this expression as \(N=N_0(0.5^{1/8})^t\text{,}\) so \(b=0.5^{1/8}=0.9170\text{,}\) and \(r=1-b=0.083\text{.}\) The decay rate is 8.3%.

Example 4.63.

The growth rate of a population of badgers is 3.8% per year. Find its doubling time.

Solution

The growth law for the population is \(P=P_0(1.038)^t\text{.}\) We set \(P=2P_0\) and solve for \(t\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] 2P_0 \amp = P_0(1.038)^t \amp\amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by}~P_0.}\\ 2 \amp = (1.038)^t \amp\amp \blert{\text{Take the log of both sides.}}\\ \log 2 \amp = t \log 1.038 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Apply the third log property.}}\\ t \amp = \dfrac{\log 2}{\log 1.038} = 18.59 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

The doubling time is 18.59 years.

Subsection 3. Analyze graphs

Subsubsection Examples

Example 4.66.

The graph shows the population, \(P\text{,}\) of a herd of llamas \(t\) years after 2000.

  1. How many llamas were there in 2000?
  2. What is the doubling time for the population?
  3. What is the annual growth rate for the population?
Solution
  1. The initial value of the population is given by the \(P\)-intercept of the graph, \((0,15)\text{.}\) There were 15 llamas in 2000.
  2. When \(t=4,~P=30\text{,}\) and when \(t=8,~P=60\text{,}\) so the llama population doubles every 4 years.
  3. The growth factor for the population is \(2^{1/4}\) or 1.189, so the annual growth rate is 18.9%.
Example 4.67.

Write a decay law for the graph shown below, where \(t\) is in hours and \(N\) is in milligrams.

Solution

The initial value is given by the vertical intercept of the graph, \((0,600)\text{,}\) so \(N_0 = 600\text{.}\)

When \(t=5,~N=300\text{,}\) so the half-life of the substance is 5 hours. Thus the decay law is \(N(t)=600(0.5)^{t/5}\text{,}\) or \(N(t)=600(0.87)^t\text{.}\)
