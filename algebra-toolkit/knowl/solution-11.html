To "undo" a square root, we square both sides of the equation. First, we isolate the square root.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \dfrac{5 \sqrt{t}}{\alert{5}} \amp = \dfrac{83}{\alert{5}} \amp \amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by 5.}}\\ (\sqrt{t})^2 \amp =(16.6)^2 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Square both sides.}}\\ t \amp =275.56 \amp \amp \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
