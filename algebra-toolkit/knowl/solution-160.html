Solution 6.29.1.

  1. \(2p=6\) and \(p^2=9\text{,}\) so \(p=\alert{3}\text{,}\) and

    \begin{equation*} x^2+6x+9 = (x+\alert{3})^2 \end{equation*}

  2. \(2p=-5\) and \(p^2=\dfrac{25}{4}\text{,}\) so \(p=\alert{\dfrac{-5}{2}}\text{,}\) and

    \begin{equation*} x^2-5x+\dfrac{25}{4} = (x-\alert{\dfrac{5}{2}})^2 \end{equation*}
