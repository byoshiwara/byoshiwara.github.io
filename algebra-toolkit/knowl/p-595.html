When we multiply a number by \(\dfrac{5}{4}\text{,}\) the product is larger than the original number, so the powers of \(\dfrac{5}{4}\) increase as the exponent increases. We can compare the powers more easily by converting the fractions to decimals, rounded to three places:

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{5}{4} = 1.25,~\dfrac{25}{16} = 1.563,~\dfrac{125}{64} = 1.953,~\dfrac{625}{256} = 2.441 \end{equation*}
