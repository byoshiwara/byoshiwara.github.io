\(\left(\dfrac{2}{3}\right)^x\) \(\dfrac{2}{3}\) \(\dfrac{4}{9}\) \(\dfrac{8}{27}\) \(\dfrac{16}{81}\)

When we multiply a number by \(\dfrac{2}{3}\text{,}\) the product is smaller than the original number, so the powers of \(\dfrac{2}{3}\) decrease as the exponent increases. We can compare the powers more easily by converting the fractions to decimals, rounded to three places:

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{2}{3} = 0.667,~\dfrac{4}{9} = 0.444,~\dfrac{8}{27} = 0.296,~\dfrac{16}{81} = 0.198 \end{equation*}
