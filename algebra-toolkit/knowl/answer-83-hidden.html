1. \(V=\pi r^2h\text{.}\) \(~~V\) stands for the volume, \(r\) for the radius, and \(h\) for the height of the cylinder.

  2. \(A=\pi r^2\text{.}\) \(~~A\) stands for the area and \(r\) for the radius of the circle.

  3. \(A=lw\text{.}\) \(~~A\) stands for the area of the rectangle, \(l\) and \(w\) stand for its length and width.

  4. \(P=2l+2w\text{.}\) \(~~P\) stands for the perimeter of the rectangle, \(l\) and \(w\) stand for its length and width.

  5. \(V=\dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3\text{.}\) \(~~V\) stands for the volume and \(r\) for the radius of the sphere.

  6. \(C=\pi d\text{.}\) \(~~C\) stands for the circumference and \(d\) for the diameter of the circle.
