If the variable is in the denominator of a fraction, we must first clear the fraction.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \alert{y}(\dfrac{15}{y}) \amp = 45 \cdot \alert{y} \amp \amp \blert{\text{Multiply both sides by} ~y.}\\ 15 \amp = 45y \amp \amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by 45.}}\\ y \amp =\dfrac{15}{45}=\dfrac{1}{3} \amp \amp \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
