We isolate the quadratic term, \(y^2\text{,}\) and then extract roots.

\begin{align*} 3y^2-40 \amp = 35 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Add 40 to both sides.}}\\ 3y^2 \amp = 75 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by 3.}}\\ y^2 \amp = 25 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Take the square root of both sides.}}\\ y \amp = \pm \sqrt{25} \amp \amp \blert{\text{Simplify.}}\\ y \amp = \pm 5 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Remeber that a positive number has two square roots.}} \end{align*}

The solutions are \(5\) and \(-5\text{.}\)
