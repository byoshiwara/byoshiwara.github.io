Subsection 1. Compute percent increase and decrease

Subsubsection Examples

Example 4.1.

A loaf of bread cost $3.00 last month, but this year the price rose by 6%. What should you multiply by to find the new price? What is the new price?

Solution

To get the new price, we multiply by 1.06 to get \(1.06(3.00)=3.18\text{.}\) The new price is $3.18.

Example 4.2.

Priceco is offering a 15% discount off the regular price of $180 for a ceiling fan. What should you multiply by to find the new price? What is the new price?

Solution

To get the new price, we multiply by \(1-0.15\text{,}\) or 0.85, to get \(0.85(180)=153\text{,}\) so the new price is $153.

Subsubsection Exercises

Answer

Answer

\(0.82\text{,}\) $28,700

Subsection 2. Use the order of operations

Subsubsection Examples

Example 4.5.

Simplify.

  1. \(\displaystyle -4-2^3\)
  2. \(\displaystyle -4(-2)^3\)
  3. \(\displaystyle (-4-2)^3\)
Solution
  1. Compute \(2^3\) first, then subtract the result from \(-4\text{:}\)
    \begin{equation*} -4-2^3=-4-8=-12 \end{equation*}
  2. Compute \((-2)^3\) first, then multiply the result by \(-4\text{:}\)
    \begin{equation*} -4(-2)^3=-4(-8)=32 \end{equation*}
  3. Compute \((-4-2)\) first, then cube the result:
    \begin{equation*} (-4-2)^3=(-6)^3=-216 \end{equation*}
Example 4.6.

Evaluate for\(x=6\text{.}\) Round your answers to hundredths.

  1. \(\displaystyle 12(1.05)^x\)
  2. \(\displaystyle 12(1+x/100)^5\)
Solution

  1. Follow the order of operations. Compute the power first:

    \begin{equation*} 12(1.05)^6 = 12(1.3400956...) = 16.08 \end{equation*}

  2. Follow the order of operations. Compute the power first:

    \begin{equation*} 12(1+6/100)^5 = 12(1.06)^5 = 12(1.3382255...) = 16.06 \end{equation*}

Subsection 3. Raise fractions to powers

Subsubsection Examples

Example 4.9.

Complete the table of powers. As the exponent increases, do the powers increase or decrease?

  1. \(~~x~~\) \(~~1~~\) \(~~2~~\) \(~~3~~\) \(~~4~~\)
    \((\dfrac{2}{3})^x\) \(\) \(\) \(\) \(\)
  2. \(~~x~~\) \(~~1~~\) \(~~2~~\) \(~~3~~\) \(~~4~~\)
    \((\dfrac{5}{4})^x\) \(\) \(\) \(\) \(\)
Solution
  1. \(~~x~~\) \(~~1~~\) \(~~2~~\) \(~~3~~\) \(~~4~~\)
    \((\dfrac{2}{3})^x\) \(\dfrac{2}{3}\) \(\dfrac{4}{9}\) \(\dfrac{8}{27}\) \(\dfrac{16}{81}\)
    When we multiply a number by \(\dfrac{2}{3}\text{,}\) the product is smaller than the original number, so the powers of \(\dfrac{2}{3}\) decrease as the exponent increases. We can compare the powers more easily by converting the fractions to decimals, rounded to three places: \(\dfrac{2}{3} = 0.667,~\dfrac{4}{9} = 0.444,~\dfrac{8}{27} = 0.296,~\dfrac{16}{81} = 0.198\text{.}\)
  2. \(~~x~~\) \(~~1~~\) \(~~2~~\) \(~~3~~\) \(~~4~~\)
    \((\dfrac{5}{4})^x\) \(\dfrac{5}{4}\) \(\dfrac{25}{16}\) \(\dfrac{125}{64}\) \(\dfrac{625}{256}\)
    When we multiply a number by \(\dfrac{5}{4}\text{,}\) the product is larger than the original number, so the powers of \(\dfrac{5}{4}\) increase as the exponent increases. We can compare the powers more easily by converting the fractions to decimals, rounded to three places: \(\dfrac{5}{4} = 1.25,~\dfrac{25}{16} = 1.563,~\dfrac{125}{64} = 1.953,~\dfrac{625}{256} = 2.441\text{.}\)
