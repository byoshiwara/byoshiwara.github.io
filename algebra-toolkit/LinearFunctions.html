Section 1.4 Section 1.5 Linear Functions

Subsection 1. Slope-Intercept Form

Slope-Intercept Form.

If we write the equation of a linear function in the form,

\begin{equation*} f (x) = b + mx \end{equation*}

then \(m\) is the slope of the line, and \(b\) is the \(y\)-intercept.

Subsubsection Examples

Example 1.48.

The temperature inside a pottery drying oven starts at 70 degrees and is rising at a rate of 0.5 degrees per minute. Write a function for the temperature, \(H\text{,}\) inside the oven after \(t\) minutes.

Solution

At \(t=0\text{,}\) the temperature is 70 degrees, so \(b=70\text{.}\) The slope is given by the rate of increase of \(H\text{,}\) so \(m=0.5\text{.}\) Thus, the function is \(H=70+0.5t\text{.}\)

Example 1.49.

A perfect score on a driving test is 120 points, and you lose 4 points for each wrong answer. Write a function for your score, \(S\text{,}\) if you give \(n\) wrong answers.

Solution

If \(n=0\text{,}\) your score is 120, so \(b=120\text{.}\) Your score decreases by 4 points per wrong answer, so \(m=\dfrac{\Delta S}{\Delta n} = 4\text{.}\) The function is \(S=120-4n\text{.}\)

Subsubsection Exercises

Notebook 1.50.

Monica has saved $7800 to live on while she attends college. She spends $600 a month. Write a function for the amount, \(S\text{,}\) in Monica's savings account after \(t\) months.

Answer
\(S=7800-600t\)
Notebook 1.51.

Jesse opened a new doughnut shop in an old store-front. He invested $2400 in remodeling and set-up, and he makes about $400 per week from the business. Write a function giving the shop's financial standing, \(F\text{,}\) after \(w\) weeks.

Answer
\(F=-2400+400w\)

Subsection 2. Point-Slope Form

Point-Slope Formula.

To find an equation for the line of slope \(m\) passing through the point \((x_1,y_1)\text{,}\) use the point-slope formula

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{y-y_1}{x-x_1} = m \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation*} y-y_1=m(x-x_1) \end{equation*}

Subsubsection Example

Example 1.52.

Find an equation for the line that passes through \((1,3)\) and has slope \(-2\text{.}\)

Solution

We substitute \(x_1=\alert{1}\text{,}\) \(y_1=\alert{3}\text{,}\) and \(m=\alert{-2}\) into the point-slope formula.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] y-\alert{3} \amp = \alert{-2} (x-\alert{1}) \amp \amp \blert{\text{Apply the distributive law.}}\\ y-3 \amp = -2x + 2 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Add 3 to both sides.}}\\ y \amp = -2x + 5 \amp \amp \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Example 1.53.

Find an equation for the line of slope \(\dfrac{-1}{2}\) that passes through \((-3,-2)\text{.}\)

Solution

We substitute \(x_1=\alert{-3}\text{,}\) \(y_1=\alert{-2}\text{,}\) and \(m=\alert{\dfrac{-1}{2}}\) into the point-slope formula.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \dfrac{y-(\alert{-2})}{x-(\alert{-3})} \amp = \alert{\dfrac{-1}{2}} \amp \amp \blert{\text{Simplify the left side.}}\\ \dfrac{y+2}{x+3} \amp = \dfrac{-1}{2} \amp \amp \blert{\text{Cross-multiply.}}\\ 2(y+2) \amp = -1(x+3) \amp \amp \blert{\text{Apply the distributive law.}}\\ 2y+4 \amp = -x-3 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Subtract 4 from both sides.}}\\ 2y \amp = -x-7 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by 2.}}\\ y \amp = \dfrac{-1}{2}x-\dfrac{7}{2} \amp \amp \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Subsubsection Exercises

Notebook 1.54.

Find an equation for the line of slope \(-4\) that passes through \((2,-5)\text{.}\)

Answer
\(y=-4x+3\)
Notebook 1.55.

Find an equation for the line of slope \(\dfrac{2}{3}\) that passes through \((-6,1)\text{.}\)

Answer
\(y=\dfrac{2}{3}x+5\)

Subsection 3. Graphing a line

Subsubsection Examples

Example 1.56.

Graph the line \(~y=\dfrac{3}{4}x-2\)

Solution

Begin by plotting the \(y\)-intercept, \((0,-2)\text{.}\) We use the slope, \(\dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{3}{4}\text{,}\) to find another point on the line, as follows. Start at the point \((0,-2)\) and move 3 units up and 4 units to the right. Plot a second point here, at \((4,1)\text{.}\)

Find a third point by writing the slope as \(\dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{-3}{-4}\text{:}\) from \((0,-2)\text{,}\) move down 3 units and 4 units to the left. Plot a third point here, at \((-4,-5)\text{.}\) Finally, draw a line through the three points.

Example 1.57.

Graph the line of slope \(\dfrac{-1}{2}\) that passes through \((-3,-2)\text{.}\)

Solution

Begin by plotting the point \((-3,-2)\text{.}\) Use the slope, \(\dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{-1}{2}\text{,}\) to find another point on the line, as follows. Start at the point \((-3,-2)\) and move 1 unit down and 3 units to the right. Plot a second point here, at \((-1,-31)\text{.}\)

Find a third point by writing the slope as \(\dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{1}{-2}\text{:}\) from \((-3,-2)\text{,}\) move 1 unit up and 2 units to the left. Plot a third point here, at \((-5,-1)\text{.}\) Finally, draw a line through the three points.
