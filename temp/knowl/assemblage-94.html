Linear Equation.

An equation of the form \(\blert{y=ax+b}\text{,}\) where \(a\) and \(b\) are constants, is called a linear equation because its graph is a straight line.

in-context =======
Sine and Cosine of Real Numbers.

We define the trigonometric functions of the number \(t\) by

\begin{equation*} \cos t = \cos \theta ~~~~\text{and} ~~~~ \sin t = \sin \theta \end{equation*}

where \(t\) is the length of the arc subtended by an angle \(\theta\text{,}\) measured in radians, on a unit circle.
