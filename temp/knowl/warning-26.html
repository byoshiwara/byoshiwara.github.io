in-context ======= Caution 8.48.

The reciprocal functions are not the same as the inverse trig functions!

For example, \(\sec 0.8\) is not equal to \(\cos^{-1}(0.8)\text{.}\) Remember that \(\cos^{-1}(0.8)\) is an angle, namely the angle whose cosine is 0.8, while \(\sec 0.8\) is the reciprocal of the cosine of 0.8 radians, or \(\dfrac{1}{\cos 0.8}\text{.}\) You can check on your calculator that

\begin{equation*} \cos^{-1}(0.8)=0.6435~\text{radians},~~~~\text{and}~~~~\sec 0.8=1.4353 \end{equation*}
