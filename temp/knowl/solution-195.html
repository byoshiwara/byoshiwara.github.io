in-context ======= Solution 10.8.1.

The polar coordinates tell us that \(r = \sqrt{2}\) and \(\theta = \dfrac{3\pi}{4}\text{,}\) as shown below.

The \(x\)-coordinate of the point is

\begin{equation*} x = r\cos \theta = \sqrt{2} \cos(\dfrac{3\pi}{4}) = \sqrt{2} \cdot \dfrac{-1}{\sqrt{2}} = -1 \end{equation*}

and its \(y\)-coordinate is

\begin{equation*} y = r\sin \theta = \sqrt{2} \sin(\dfrac{3\pi}{4}) = \sqrt{2} \cdot \dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2}} = 1 \end{equation*}

Thus, the Cartesian coordinates are \((-1,1)\text{.}\)
