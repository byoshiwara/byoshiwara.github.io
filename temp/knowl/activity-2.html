  13. You have proven Heron's formula.

    Heron's formula.

    Suppose that \(\Delta ABC\) has sides of length \(a\text{,}\) \(b\text{,}\) and \(c\text{.}\) Then the area of \(\Delta ABC\) is given by

    \begin{equation*} A=\sqrt{s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)} \end{equation*}

    where \(s=\dfrac{a+b+c}{2}\) is the semiperimeter.
