Point-Slope Formula.

To find an equation for the line of slope \(m\) passing through the point \((x_1,y_1)\text{,}\) we use the point-slope formula

\begin{equation*} \blert{\dfrac{y-y_1}{x-x_1} = m} \end{equation*}

or

\begin{equation*} \blert{y-y_1 = m(x-x_1)} \end{equation*}

Polar Coordinates.

The polar coordinates of a point \(P\) in the plane are \((r, \theta)\text{,}\) where

  • \(\abs{r}\) is the distance from to the pole,

  • \(\theta\) is the angle measured counterclockwise from the polar axis to the ray through \(P\) from the pole.
