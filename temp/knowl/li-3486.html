Pythagorean Theorem.

If \(c\) stands for the length of the hypotenuse of a right triangle, and the lengths of the two legs are represented by \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} a^2 + b^2 = c^2 \end{equation*}
