We multiply both sides of the equation by \(x\text{,}\) the denominator of the fraction, to get

\begin{align*} \cancel{\blert{x}}(\dfrac{5000+50x}{\cancel{x}}) \amp = (75)\blert{x}\\ 5000+50x \amp = 75x \end{align*}

Then we proceed as usual to finish the solution. We subtract \(50x\) from both sides to find

\begin{align*} 5000 \amp = 25x\\ 200 \amp = x \end{align*}

You can check that \(x=200\) does satisfy the equation.

The letter \(i\) used in this way is not a variable, it is the name of a specific number, and hence is a constant. The square root of any negative number can be written as the product of a real number and \(i\). For example,

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \sqrt{-4} \amp = \sqrt{-1 \cdot 4}\\ \amp = \sqrt{-1} \sqrt{4} = i \cdot 2\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

or \(\sqrt{-4} = 2\text{.}\) Any number that is the product of \(i\) and a real number is called an imaginary number.
