Solve \(~~\dfrac{3}{4} = 8- \dfrac{2x+11}{x-5}\)

in-context =======

Consider the quadratic equation

\begin{equation*} x^2 - 2x + 5 = 0 \end{equation*}

Using the quadratic formula to solve the equation, we find

\begin{equation*} x=\dfrac{-(-2) \pm \sqrt{(-2)^2-4(1)(5)}}{2} = \dfrac{2 \pm \sqrt{-16}}{2} \end{equation*}

If we now replace \(\sqrt{-16}\) by \(4i\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation*} x = \dfrac{2 \pm 4i}{2} = 1 \pm 2i \end{equation*}

The two solutions are \(1 + 2i\) and \(1 - 2i\text{.}\) These are examples of complex numbers.
