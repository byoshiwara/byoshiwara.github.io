Example: Solve \(~~\dfrac{-3t}{4} = 6\)

  1. Analyze the expression \(~4x-5\text{.}\)

    \begin{align*} \amp \blert{\text{Operations performed on}~x} \amp \amp \blert{\text{Steps for solution}}\\ \amp 1.~~~ \text{Multiply by}~-3 \amp \amp 1.~~~ \text{Multiply by 4}\\ \amp 2.~~~ \text{Divide by 4} \amp \amp 2.~~~\text{Divide by} ~-3 \end{align*}

  2. Carry out the solution.

    \begin{align*} \dfrac{-3t}{4} \amp = 6 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Multiply both sides by 4.}}\\ \alert{4}\left(\dfrac{-3t}{4}\right) \amp = (6) \alert{4}\\ -3t \amp = 24 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by}~-3}\\ \dfrac{-3t}{\alert{-3}} \amp = \dfrac{24}{\alert{-3}}\\ x \amp = 3 \end{align*}

    The solutions is \(-8\text{.}\) \(~~~~~~~~\blert{\text{Check:}}~~~~\dfrac{-3(\alert{-8})}{4} = 6\)
