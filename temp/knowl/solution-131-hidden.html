You may notice that each term of this equation is twice the corresponding term of the equation in Example 6.34. We can factor out a common factor of 2 from the left side of the standard form of this equation.

\begin{align*} 2x^2+6x-36 \amp =0 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Factor out 2 from the left side.}}\\ 2(x^2+3x-18) \amp = 0 \end{align*}

Then we can divide both sides of the equation by 2.

\begin{align*} \dfrac{2(x^2+3x-18)}{\blert{2}} \amp = \dfrac{0}{\blert{2}}\\ x^2+3x-18 \amp = 0 \end{align*}

This new equation is the same as the equation in Example 6.34. Thus, the solutions are the same as the solutions in Example 6.34, namely, \(-6\) and \(3\text{.}\)
