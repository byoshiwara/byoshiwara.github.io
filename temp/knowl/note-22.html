in-context ======= Note 5.29.

In the previous example, notice that the solutions are \(180\degree\) apart. The solutions of an equation \(\tan \theta = k\) always differ by \(180\degree\text{,}\) so the use of reference angles is not necessary for these equations: once we have found one solution, we can add or subtract \(180\degree\) to find the other solution between \(0\degree\) and \(360\degree\text{.}\) (See Homework Problems 69 and 70.)
