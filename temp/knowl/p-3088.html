We substitute \(\sin \theta = \dfrac{7}{25}\) into the Pythagorean identity \(\cos^2 \theta + \sin^2 \theta = 1\) to get

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \cos^2 \theta + (\dfrac{7}{25})^2 \amp = 1 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Isolate } \cos^2 \theta.}\\ \cos^2 \theta \amp = 1 - (\dfrac{7}{25})^2 \amp \amp \textstyle{\blert{1 - (\frac{7}{25})^2 = 1 - \frac{49}{625} = \frac{625-49}{625}}}\\ \cos^2 \theta \amp = \dfrac{576}{625} \amp \amp \blert{\text{Take square roots.}}\\ \cos \theta \amp = \pm \sqrt{\dfrac{576}{625}} = \pm \dfrac{24}{25}\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Because \(\theta\) is between \(90\degree\) and \(180\degree\text{,}\) \(\cos \theta\) must be negative, so \(\cos \theta = \dfrac{-24}{25}\text{.}\) To find \(\tan \theta\text{,}\) we use the tangent identity.

\begin{equation*} \tan \theta = \dfrac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta} = \dfrac{7/25}{-24/25} = \dfrac{-7}{24} \end{equation*}
