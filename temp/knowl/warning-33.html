<<<<<<< HEAD Caution 5.4.

Note that \(3^4\) does not mean 3 times 4 or 12; we already have a way to write \(3(4)\text{.}\) Remember that an exponent or power indicates repeated multiplication of the base.

in-context ======= Caution 10.30.

Solving the system of equations \(r=f(\theta)\) and \(r=g(\theta)\) will not always reveal an intersection at the pole, because \(r\) may be equal to zero for different values of \(\theta\) in the two equations. Consequently, we should always check separately whether the pole is a point on both graphs.
