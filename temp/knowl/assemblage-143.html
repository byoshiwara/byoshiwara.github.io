Equation for Proportional Variables.

If two variables are proportional, they are related by the equation

\begin{equation*} y=kx \end{equation*}

where \(k\) is the constant of proportionality.

in-context =======
Inverse Trigonometric Functions.
\begin{equation*} \sin(\sin^{-1}x) = x~~~~\text{for}~ -1 \le x \le 1 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \cos(\cos^{-1}x) = x~~~~\text{for}~ -1 \le x \le 1 \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \tan(\tan^{-1}x) = x~~~~\text{for all}~x \end{equation*}

However,

\begin{equation*} \sin^{-1}(\sin x)~~\text{may not be equal to}~x \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \cos^{-1}(\cos x)~~\text{may not be equal to}~x \end{equation*}
\begin{equation*} \tan^{-1}(\tan x)~~\text{may not be equal to}~x \end{equation*}
