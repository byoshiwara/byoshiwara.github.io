Look Closer.

What about the equation of a vertical line? The slope of a vertical line is undefined; a vertical line does not have a slope. We cannot use the slope-intercept form to write the equation of a vertical line. However, we can use what we learned about horizontal lines in ExampleÂ 3.63.

Look at the graph of the vertical line at right. Every point on the line has \(x\)-coordinate \(-2\text{,}\) no matter what the \(y\)-coordinate is. An equation for this line is \(x=-2\text{.}\) The value of \(x\) does not depend upon \(y\text{;}\) it is constant, so \(y\) does not appear in the equation.
