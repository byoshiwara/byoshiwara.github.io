We replace \(\sec \theta\) by \(\dfrac{1}{\cos \theta}\) and \(\tan \theta\) by \(\dfrac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta}\) to get

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \dfrac{1}{\cos \theta} - \dfrac{\sin \theta}{\cos \theta} \cdot \sin \theta \amp = \dfrac{1}{\cos \theta} - \dfrac{\sin^2 \theta}{\cos \theta}\\ \amp = \dfrac{1 - \sin^2 \theta}{\cos \theta} = \dfrac{\cos^2 \theta}{\cos \theta} = \cos \theta\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
