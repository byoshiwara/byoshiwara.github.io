Write each expression as a single fraction in simplest form.

in-context =======

Because \(i\) is really a radical (remember that \(i=\sqrt{-1}\)), we multiply the numerator and denominator of the quotient by \(i\) to obtain

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \dfrac{(3+4i) \cdot i}{2i \cdot i}\amp= \dfrac{3i+4i^2}{2i^2} \amp \amp \blert{\text{Apply the distributive law.}}\\ \amp=\drac{3i-4}{-2} \amp \amp \blert{\text{Recall that} i^2=-1.}\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

To write the quotient in the form \(a+bi>\text{,}\) we divide \(-2\) into each term of the numerator.

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{3i}{-2} - \dfrac{4}{-2} = \dfrac{-3}{2}i +2 \end{equation*}

The quotient is \(2 - \dfrac{3}{2} i\text{.}\)
