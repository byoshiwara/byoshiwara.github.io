Addition and Subtraction Properties of Equality.

If the same quantity is added to or subtracted from both sides of an equation, the solution is unchanged. In symbols,

\begin{equation*} \blert{\text{If}~~a=b,~~ \text{then}~~a+c=b+c~~\text{and}~~a-c=b-c} \end{equation*}
in-context =======
Area of a Triangle.

If a triangle has sides of length \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) and the angle between those two sides is \(\theta\text{,}\) then the area of the triangle is given by

\begin{equation*} A = \dfrac{1}{2} ab \sin \theta \end{equation*}
