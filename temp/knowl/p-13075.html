For any base \(b \gt 0, b \ne 1\text{:}\)

  1. The logarithmic function \(y = \log_b x\) is defined for positive \(x\) only.
  2. The \(x\)-intercept of its graph is \((1,0)\text{.}\)
  3. The graph has a vertical asymptote at \(x=0\text{.}\)
  4. The graphs of \(y = \log_b x\) and \(y=b^x\) are symmetric about the line \(y=x\text{.}\)
