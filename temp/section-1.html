Activity 1.2. Writing Mathematical Sentences.

  1. Barry lives with his aunt while he attends college. Every week he gives her $20 from his paycheck to help pay for groceries. Fill in the table:

    Barry's paycheck 45 60 75 100 125 \(p\)
    Calculation \(45-20\) \(\hphantom{60-20 }\) \(\hphantom{75-20 }\) \(\hphantom{100-20 }\) \(\hphantom{125-20 }\) \(\hphantom{p - 20 }\)
    Amount he keeps \(25\)

    1. Explain in words how to find the amount Barry keeps from his paycheck.

    2. Write your explanation as a mathematical sentence:

      \begin{equation*} \text{Amount he keeps} =\phantom{Amount he keeps} \end{equation*}

    3. Let \(p\) stand for the amount of Barry's paycheck and \(k\) for the amount he keeps. Write an equation for \(k\) in terms of \(p\text{.}\)

    4. Plot the points from the table and connect them with a smooth curve.

    1. Liz makes $6 an hour as a tutor in the Math Lab. Her wages for the week depend on the number of hours she works. Fill in the table.

      Hours worked 3 5 6 8 15 \(h\)
      Calculation \(6 \times 3\) \(\hphantom{6 \times 3 }\) \(\hphantom{6 \times 3}\) \(\hphantom{6 \times 3 }\) \(\hphantom{6 \times 15}\) \(\hphantom{6h }\)
      Wages \(18\)

      1. Explain in words how to find Liz's wages for the week.

      2. Write your explanation as a mathematical sentence:

        \begin{equation*} \text{Wages} =\phantom{Amount she earns} \end{equation*}

      3. Let \(h\) stand for the number of hours Liz worked and \(w\) for her wages. Write an equation for \(w\) in terms of \(h\text{.}\)

      4. Plot the points from the table and connect them with a smooth curve.
