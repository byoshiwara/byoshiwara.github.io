Pascal's triangle has other interesting properties besides providing the binomial coefficients.

  3. Now add entries of Pascal's triangle along the diagonal lines indicated below. Fill in two more rows of Pascal's triangle and find two more diagonal sums.

  4. Write the sequence of numbers obtained by taking sums along the diagonal lines. What is the name of this sequence?

    The binomial coefficient \(~_nC_k~\) also gives the number of different ways you can choose \(k\) distinct items from a set of \(n\) items. Finding these numbers is important in determining the probability that certain events will occur.

  5. Find the number of different choices you can make for a four-flavor ice cream sundae if there are six flavors to choose from. (Caution: When you are deciding which entry in the row corresponds to \(k=4\text{,}\) remember that the left-most entry corresponds to \(k=0\text{.}\))

  6. How many ways can you choose a debate team of three members from a debate club of 5 members?

  7. Evaluate the series \(~~\displaystyle{\sum_{k=0}^{10} ~_{10}C_r~} \text{.}\)

    (Hint: This is the sum of all the binomial coefficients in the expansion of \((a+b)^n\text{.}\) What did you learn in Step 2 about the sum of the terms in a row of Pascal's triangle?)

  8. Evaluate the series \(~~\displaystyle{\sum_{k=0}^{12} ~_{12}C_r~} \text{.}\)

  9. How many ways can you choose 6 objects from a set of 49? (This is the number of possible choices in a lottery where you pick 6 numbers from 1 to 49.)

  10. How many ways can you choose 5 cards out of a deck of 52 distinct cards? (This is the number of different poker hands.)
