Write the equation in standard form as

\begin{equation*} 2x^2 - 4x + 1 = 0 \end{equation*}

Substitute \(\alert{2}\) for \(a\text{,}\) \(\alert{-4}\) for \(b\text{,}\) and \(\alert{1}\) for \(c\) into the quadratic formula, then simplify. \begin{align*} x \amp =\frac{-(\alert{-4})\pm \sqrt{(\alert{-4})^2 - 4(\alert{2})(\alert{1})}}{2(\alert{2})} \\ \amp = \frac{4 \pm \sqrt{8}}{4} \end{align*} Using a calculator, we find that the solutions are approximately \(1.707\) and \(0.293\text{.}\) (If you would like to review following the order of operations on a calculator, please see Appendix B.)
