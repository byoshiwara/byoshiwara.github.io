Exercise 20.

A florist finds that she will sell \(\dfrac{300}{x} \) dozen roses per week if she charges \(x\) dollars for a dozen. Her suppliers will sell her \(5x - 55\) dozen roses if she sells them at \(x\) dollars per dozen.

  1. Graph the demand function, \(D(x)= \dfrac{300}{x} \text{,}\) and the supply function, \(S(x) = 5x -55\text{,}\) in the same window. At what equilibrium price \(x\) will the florist sell all the roses she purchases?

  2. Write and solve an equation to verify your equilibrium price.
