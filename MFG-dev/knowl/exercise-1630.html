Exercise 37.

Before the advent of antibiotics, an outbreak of cholera might spread through a city so that the number of cases doubled every \(6\) days.
  1. Twenty-six cases were discovered on July 5. Write a function for the number of cases of cholera \(t\) days later.
  2. Use your calculator to graph your function on the interval \(0 \le t\le 90\text{.}\)
  3. When should hospitals expect to be treating \(106,496\) cases? Use algebraic methods to find your answer, and verify it on your graph.
