Item 6.23.a

Follow the steps to solve by completing the square: \(x^2 - 1 = 3x\text{.}\)

  1. Write the equation with the constant on the right.

  2. Complete the square on the left:

    \begin{equation*} p = \frac{1}{2}(-3) ={}\underline{\hspace{2.727272727272727em}}\text{, } ~~~{} p^2 = \underline{\hspace{2.727272727272727em}} \end{equation*}

    Add \(p^2\) to both sides.

  3. Write the left side as a perfect square; simplify the right side.

  4. Solve by extracting roots.
