The sequence is geometric with \(a_1 = 5\text{.}\) We find by dividing \(\dfrac{10}{3}\) by 5.

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{10}{3} \div 5 = \dfrac{2}{3} \end{equation*}

Thus, the general term of the series is \(a_n = 5(\dfrac{2}{3})^{n-1}\text{.}\) We use the formula for a geometric series with \(n=5\text{,}\)

\begin{equation*} S_5 = \dfrac{a_6 - a_1}{r-1} \end{equation*}

We substitute \(a_1 = 5,~a_6 = 5(\dfrac{2}{3})^5\text{,}\) and \(r=\dfrac{2}{3}\text{.}\) Thus

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] S_5 \amp = \dfrac{5(\dfrac{2}{3})^5 - 5}{\dfrac{2}{3}-1} = \dfrac{5(\dfrac{32}{243} - 1)}{-\dfrac{1}{3}}\\ \amp = 5(\dfrac{32-243}{243})(\dfrac{-3}{1}) \approx 13.02\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
