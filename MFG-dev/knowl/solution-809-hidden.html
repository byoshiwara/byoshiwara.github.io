1. We are looking for the equilibrium price, \(p\text{.}\)
  2. The Coffee Connection would like the demand for its coffee to equal its supply. We equate the expressions for supply and for demand to obtain the equation
    \begin{equation*} 800 - 60p = 175 + 40p \end{equation*}
  3. Solve the equation. To get all terms containing the variable, \(p\text{,}\) on one side of the equation, we add \(60p\) to both sides and subtract \(175\) from both sides to obtain
    \begin{align*} 800 - 60p + \alert{60p - 175} \amp= 175 + 40p + \alert{60p - 175}\\ 625 \amp = 100p \amp\amp\blert{\text{Divide both sides by }100.}\\ 6.25 \amp = p \end{align*}
  4. The Coffee Connection should charge $\(6.25\) per pound for its coffee.
