Exercise 83.

Archaeopteryx is an extinct creature with characteristics of both birds and reptiles. Only six fossil specimens are known, and only five of those include both a femur (leg bone) and a humerus (forearm bone) The scatterplot shows the lengths of femur and humerus for the five Archaeopteryx specimens.

  1. Use a straightedge to draw a line that fits the data.

  2. Predict the humerus length of an Archaeopteryx whose femur is \(40\) centimeters.

  3. Predict the humerus length of an Archaeopteryx whose femur is \(75\) centimeters.

  4. Use your answers from parts (b) and (c) to approximate the equation of a regression line.

  5. Use your answer to part (d) to predict the humerus length of an Archaeopteryx whose femur is \(60\) centimeters.

  6. Use your calculator and the given points on the scatterplot to find the least squares regression line. Compare the score this equation gives for part (d) with what you predicted earlier. The ordered pairs defining the data are \((38, 41)\text{,}\) \((56, 63)\text{,}\) \((59, 70)\text{,}\) \((64, 72)\text{,}\) \((74, 84)\text{.}\)
