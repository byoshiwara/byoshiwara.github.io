The surface area of a cylinder is a function of its diameter and height,

\begin{equation*} S = f (d, h) = \pi dh + \frac{\pi}{2}d^2 \end{equation*}

  1. What is the surface area of a cylindrical oatmeal container with diameter \(4\) inches and height \(7\) inches? Write your answer with function notation.

  2. Write a formula in terms of \(d\) for the surface area of a cylinder whose height is equal to its diameter.

  3. How does the surface area of the cylinder in part (b) compare with the surface area of a sphere of the same diameter? Sketch both surfaces with the same center.
