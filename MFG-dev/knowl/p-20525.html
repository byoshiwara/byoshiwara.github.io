Although we cannot take the square root of a negative number, we can take the cube root of any real number. For example,
\begin{equation*} \sqrt[3]{64}=4 ~~\text{ because }~~ 4^3=64 \end{equation*}
and
\begin{equation*} \sqrt[3]{-27}=-3 ~~\text{ because }~~ (-3)^3=-27 \end{equation*}
