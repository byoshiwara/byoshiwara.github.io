1. One-half of \(-12\) is \(-6\text{,}\) so the constant term is \((-6)^2\text{,}\) or \(36\text{.}\) Add \(36\) to obtain \begin{equation*} x^2 - 12x \alert{{}+{}36}=(x - 6)^2~~~~~~~  \underset{p^2 = (-6)^2 = 36}{\stackrel {p = \frac{1}{2}(-12) = -6}{}} \end{equation*}

One-half of \(5\) is \(\dfrac{5}{2}\text{,}\) so the constant term is \(\left(\dfrac{5}{2}\right)^2\text{,}\) or \(\dfrac{25}{4}\text{.}\) Add \(\dfrac{25}{4}\) to obtain \begin{equation*} x^2 +5x \alert{{}+{}\frac{25}{4}}=\left(x +\frac{5}{2}\right)^2~~~~~~~  \underset{p^2 = (\frac{5}{2})^2 = \frac{25}{4}}{\stackrel {p = \frac{1}{2}(5) = \frac{5}{2}}{}} \end{equation*}
