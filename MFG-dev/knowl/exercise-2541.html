Exercise 48.

  1. Graph the functions in the same window on your calculator:
    \begin{gather*} F(x) = x-\frac{1}{2} x^2 ,~~~ G(x) = 3x-\frac{1}{2} x^2,\\ H(x) = 5x-\frac{1}{2} x^2,~~~ J(x) =7x-\frac{1}{2} x^2 \end{gather*}
  2. Find the vertex of each graph in part (a) and plot the points.
  3. Find the equation of the curve in part (b).
  4. Show that the vertex of \(y =kx-\dfrac{1}{2} x^2\) lies on the curve for any value of \(k\text{.}\)
