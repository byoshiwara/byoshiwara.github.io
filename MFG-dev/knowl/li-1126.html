We see that the slope of the line is \(m = \dfrac{4}{3}\) and its \(y\)-intercept is \(b = -2\text{.}\) We begin by plotting the \(y\)-intercept, \((0, -2)\text{.}\) We then use the slope to find another point on the line. We have \begin{equation*} m = \frac{\Delta y}{\Delta x}=\frac{4}{3} \end{equation*} so starting at \((0, -2)\text{,}\) we move \(4\) units in the \(y\)-direction and \(3\) units in the \(x\)-direction, to arrive at the point \((3, 2)\text{.}\) Finally, we draw the line through these two points.
