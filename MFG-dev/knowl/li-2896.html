Let \(S\) represent Imogen’s sales, and let \(C(S)\) represent her commission. Then

\begin{equation*} C(S) = 0.15S \end{equation*}

Thus, \(C(S)\) is a linear function of \(S\) with a \(C\)-intercept of zero, so Imogen's earnings do vary directly with her sales. This is an example of direct variation. (See Figure 3.9b.)
