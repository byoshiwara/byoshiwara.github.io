We denote the interval \(~x\lt 3~\) by \((-\infty, 3)\text{,}\) and the interval \(~x\ge -1~\) by \([-1, \infty)\text{.}\) The symbol \(\infty\text{,}\) for infinity, does not represent a specific real number; it indicates that the interval continues forever along the real line.
