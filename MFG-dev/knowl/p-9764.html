First, we divide each side by \(20\) to obtain

\begin{equation*} 7 = e^{0.4x} \end{equation*}

Then we convert the equation to logarithmic form.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] 0.4x \amp = \ln 7 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by 0.4.}}\\ x \amp= \frac{\ln 7}{0.4} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Rounded to four decimal places, \(x \approx 4.8648\text{.}\)
