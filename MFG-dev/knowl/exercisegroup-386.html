Problems 53-56 give examples of functions whose graphs have holes.
  1. Find the domain of the function.
  2. Reduce the fraction to lowest terms.
  3. Graph the function. (Hint: The graph of the original function is identical to the graph of the function in part (b) except that certain points are excluded from the domain.) Indicate a hole in the graph by an open circle.

55.
\(y=\dfrac{x+1}{x^2-1} \)
56.
\(y=\dfrac{x-3}{x^2-9} \)
