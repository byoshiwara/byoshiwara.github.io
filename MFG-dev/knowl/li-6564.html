Item b
One-half of \(5\) is \(\dfrac{5}{2}\text{,}\) so the constant term is \(\left(\dfrac{5}{2}\right)^2\text{,}\) or \(\dfrac{25}{4}\text{.}\) Add \(\dfrac{25}{4}\) to obtain
\begin{equation*} x^2 +5x \alert{{}+{}\frac{25}{4}}=\left(x +\frac{5}{2}\right)^2~~~~~~~  \underset{p^2 = (\frac{5}{2})^2 = \frac{25}{4}}{\stackrel {p = \frac{1}{2}(5) = \frac{5}{2}}{}} \end{equation*}
