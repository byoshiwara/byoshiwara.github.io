Example C.32.

Find all the intersection points between the graphs of \(y=x+6\) and \(y=x^2\text{.}\)

We enter \(~ x +6 ~\) ⮠ to graph the first equation, then \(~ x~\text{,}\) the squaring key, ⮠ to graph the second equation.

We can minimize the Algebra View and then click on each intersection point to see its coordinates. The two intersection points are \((-2,4)\) and \((3,9)\text{.}\)
