By rewriting expressions as fractions, verify that the laws of exponents hold for negative exponents. Show where you apply the corresponding law for positive exponents. Here is the fourth law as an example:

\begin{align*} (ab)^{-3} \amp= \frac{1}{(ab)^3}= \frac{1}{a^3b^3} \amp\amp\text{By the fourth law of exponents.}\\ \amp= \frac{1}{a^3}\cdot\frac{1}{b^3}=a^{-3}b^{-3} \end{align*} in-context
