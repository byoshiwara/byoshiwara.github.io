  1. We represent the measure of each angle by a separate variable.

    \begin{align*} \amp\text{First angle: }\amp\amp x\\ \amp\text{Second angle: }\amp\amp y\\ \amp\text{Third angle:}\amp\amp z \end{align*}

  2. We write the conditions stated in the problem as three equations.

    \begin{gather*} x = 2y - 4\\ z = x + y + 20\\ x + y + z = 180 \end{gather*}

    (The third equation states the fact that the sum of the angles of a triangle is \(180\degree\text{.}\))

  3. We follow the steps for solving a \(3\times 3\) linear system.

    i. We write the three equations in standard form.

    \begin{alignat*}{5} x\amp {}-{}\amp 2y\amp {}{}\amp \amp =\amp -4\amp\hphantom{blank} \amp(1)\\ x\amp {}+{}\amp y\amp {}-{}\amp z\amp =\amp -20\amp\hphantom{blank} \amp(2)\\ x\amp {}+{}\amp y\amp {}+{}\amp z\amp =\amp 180\amp\hphantom{blank} \amp(3) \end{alignat*}

    ii-iii. Since Equation (1) has no \(z\)-term, it will be most efficient to eliminate the variable \(z\) from Equations (2) and (3). We add these two equations.

    \(x\) \(+\) \(y\) \(-\) \(z\) \(=\) \(-20\) \((2)\)
    \(x\) \(+\) \(y\) \(+\) \(2z\) \(=\) \(4\) \((3)\)
    \(2x\) \(+\) \(2y\) \(\) \(\) \(=\) \(160\) \((4)\)

    iv. We form a \(2\times 2\) system from Equations (1) and (4). We add the two equations to eliminate the variable \(y\text{,}\) yielding

    \(x\) \(-\) \(2y\) \(=\) \(-4\) \((1)\)
    \(2x\) \(+\) \(2y\) \(=\) \(160\) \((4)\)
    \(3x\) \(\) \(\) \(=\) \(156\) \((5)\)

    v. We form a triangular system using Equations (3), (1), and (5). We use back-substitution to complete the solution.

    \begin{alignat*}{4} x\amp {}+{}\amp y\amp {}+{}\amp z\amp =\amp 180\hphantom{blankblank} \amp(2)\\ x\amp {}-{}\amp 2y\amp \amp\amp =\amp -4\hphantom{blankblank} \amp(1)\\ 3x\amp\amp\amp\amp\amp = \amp156\hphantom{blankblank} \amp(5) \end{alignat*}

    We divide both sides of Equation (5) by \(3\) to find \(x = 52\text{.}\) We substitute \(52\) for \(x\) in Equation (1) and solve for \(y\) to find

    \begin{align*} \alert{52} - 2y \amp = -4\\ y \amp= 28 \end{align*}

    We substitute \(52\) for \(x\) and \(28\) for \(y\) in Equation (3) to find

    \begin{align*} \alert{52} + \blert{28} + z \amp= 180\\ z\amp=100 \end{align*}

  4. The angles measure \(52\degree\text{,}\) \(28\degree\text{,}\) and \(100\degree\text{.}\)
