We substitute \(64\) for \(h\) in the formula, and solve for \(t\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] 64 = -16t^2 + 64t \amp + 4\amp\amp \blert{\text{Write the equation in standard form.}}\\ 16t^2 - 64t + 60 \amp= 0\amp\amp \blert{\text{Factor 4 from the left side.}}\\ 4(4t^2 - 16t + 15) \amp= 0\amp\amp \blert{\text{Factor the quadratic expression.}}\\ 4(2t - 3)(2t - 5) \amp= 0\amp\amp \blert{\text{Set each variable factor equal to zero.}}\\ 2t - 3 =0 ~~\text{ or }~~ 2t - 5 \amp= 0\amp\amp \blert{\text{Solve each equation.}}\\ t = \frac{3}{2} ~~\text{ or }~~ t \amp = \frac{5}{2} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

There are two solutions to the quadratic equation. At \(t = \dfrac{3}{2}\) seconds, the ball reaches a height of \(64\) feet on the way up, and at \(t = \dfrac{5}{2}\) seconds, the ball is \(64\) feet high on its way down.
