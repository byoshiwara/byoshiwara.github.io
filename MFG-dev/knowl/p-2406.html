  1. Linear functions form a two-parameter family, \(f (x) = b + mx\text{.}\)
  2. The initial value of a linear function and the \(y\)-intercept of its graph are given by \(b\text{.}\) The rate of change of the function and the slope of its graph are given by \(m\text{.}\)
  3. The slope-intercept form, \(y = b + mx\text{,}\) is useful when we know the initial value and the rate of change.
  4. The point-slope form, \(y = y_1 + m(x - x_1)\text{,}\) is useful when we know the rate of change and one point on the line.
