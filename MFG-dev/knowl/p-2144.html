The distance (in miles) covered by a cross-country competitor is given by \(d = 6t\text{,}\) where \(t\) is the number of hours she runs.

  1. Make a table of values for the function.

    \(t\) \(2\) \(4\) \(6\) \(8\)
    \(d\) \(\hphantom{0000} \) \(\hphantom{0000}\) \(\hphantom{0000}\) \(\hphantom{0000}\)

  2. Graph the function.

  3. Using two points on the graph, compute the slope \(\dfrac{\Delta d}{\Delta t}\text{,}\) including units.

  4. What does the slope tell us about the cross-country runner?
