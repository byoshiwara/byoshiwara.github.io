We cannot take an even root of a negative number. (See A Note on Roots of Negative Numbers "A Note on Roots of Negative Numbers" at the end of this section.) Hence, if \(n\) is even, the domain of \(f (x) = x^{1/n}\) is restricted to nonnegative real numbers, but if \(n\) is odd, the domain of \(f (x) = x^{1/n}\) is the set of all real numbers.
