We can check the approximations found in Example 4.38 with our conversion equations. Remember that a logarithm is an exponent, and in this example the base is \(10\text{.}\) We find that \​begin{align*} \amp\amp 10^{0.81} \amp\approx 6.45654229 \\ \text{and} \amp\amp 10^{2.41} \amp\approx 257.0395783 \end{align*} so our approximations are reasonable, although you can see that rounding a logarithm to \(2\) decimal places does lose some accuracy.
