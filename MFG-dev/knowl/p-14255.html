Consider the quadratic equation
\begin{equation*} y = 2(x - 3)^2 - 8 \end{equation*}
By expanding the squared expression and collecting like terms, we can rewrite the equation in standard form as
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] y \amp= 2(x^2 - 6x + 9) - 8\\ y \amp = 2x^2 - 12x + 10 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
The vertex of this parabola is
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] x_v \amp= \frac{-(-12)}{2(2)}=3\\ y_v \amp = 2(3)^2 - 12(3) + 10=-8 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
