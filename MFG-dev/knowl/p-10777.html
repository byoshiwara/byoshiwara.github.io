  • The rectangle with length \(x + 10\) and width \(x\) has area \(x(x + 10) = x^2 + 10x\text{,}\) as shown in Figure 6.26a.
  • We would like to cut the rectangle into pieces and rearrange them so that we can make a square.
  • In Figure 6.26b, we move half of the \(x\)-term so that each side of the square has length \(x + 5\) (note that \(p = \frac{1}{2} (10) = 5\)).
  • In Figure 6.26c we see that the missing corner piece has area \(p^2 = 5^2 = 25\text{.}\)
