The relationship in Example 4.31 holds true for all increasing power and exponential functions: For large enough values of \(x\text{,}\) the exponential function will always be greater than the power function, regardless of the parameters in the functions. Figure 4.35 shows the graphs of \(f(x) = x^6\) and \(g(x) = 1.8^x\text{.}\) At first, \(f (x) \gt g(x)\text{,}\) but at around \(x = 37\text{,}\) \(g(x)\) overtakes \(f (x)\text{,}\) and \(g(x) \gt f (x)\) for all \(x \gt 37\text{.}\)
