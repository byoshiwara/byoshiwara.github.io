Item 1.24.b.

What do the intercepts tell us about the problem?

The \(x\)-intercept tells us:

  • the cost of the TV ads.

  • the best number of TV ads.

  • the most TV ads she can buy for the money.

  • the average number of TV ads.

The \(y\)-intercept tells us:

  • the cost of the radio ads.

  • the number of radio ads she bought

  • the number of radio ads if no TV ads are bought.

  • the average cost per ad.
