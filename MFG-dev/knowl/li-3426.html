Item a.

Because \(v\) varies directly with \(t\text{,}\) there is a positive constant \(k\) for which \(v = kt\text{.}\) We substitute \(v = 39.2\) when \(t = 4\) and solve for \(k\) to find

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] 39.2 \amp = k(4)\amp\amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by 4.}}\\ k \amp = 9.8 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Thus, \(v (t) = 9.8 t\text{.}\)
