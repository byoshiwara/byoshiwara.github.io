We can also use graphs to solve absolute value inequalities. Look again at the graph of \(y = \abs{3x - 6}\) in FigureÂ 2.120a. Because of the V-shape of the graph, all points with \(y\)-values less than \(9\) lie between the two solutions of \(\abs{3x - 6} = 9\text{,}\) that is, between \(-1\) and \(5\text{.}\) Thus, the solutions of the inequality \(\abs{3x - 6} \lt 9\) are \(-1 \lt x \lt 5\text{.}\) (In the Homework problems, you will be asked to show this algebraically.)
