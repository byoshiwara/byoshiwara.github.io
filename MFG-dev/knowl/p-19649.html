We can now find a non-recursive formula for the general term of a geometric sequence. If we denote the first term of the geometric sequence by \(a\text{,}\) then the
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \text{second term is}~~~~~~ \amp ar\\ \text{third term is}~~~~~~ \amp ar \cdot r = ar^2\\ \text{fourth term is}~~~~~~ \amp ar^2 \cdot r = ar^3\\ n^{\text{th}}~ \text{term is}~~~~~~ \amp ar^{n-1}\\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
