Project 9.7. A Logistic Model.

The model in the previous project is only useful for a limited time, because the fish population cannot continue to grow indefinitely. The model described below is called a logistic model. Suppose a fishery starts with a stock of 20 fish, so \(S_0 = 20\text{.}\) The growth \(G_n\) of the population in year \(n\) is given by

\begin{equation*} G_n = 0.001 S_n (400-S_n) \end{equation*}

and

\begin{equation*} S_n = S_{n-1} + G_{n-1} \end{equation*}

  1. Make a table showing values of \(S_n\) and \(G_n\) up to \(n=20\text{,}\) rounding values of \(G_n\) to whole numbers.

  2. Plot the values of \(G_n\text{.}\) What is the shape of the graph? In what year does the fish population grow the most?

  3. Plot the values of \(S_n\text{.}\) What is the limiting value of the fish population? Describe the growth of the population.
