Exercise 13.

The risk magnitude of an event is defined by \(R = 10+ \log p\text{,}\) where \(p\) is the probability of the event occurring. Calculate the probability of each event.
  1. The sun will rise tomorrow, \(R = 10\text{.}\)
  2. The next child born in Arizona will be a boy, \(R = 9.7\text{.}\)
  3. A major hurricane will strike North Carolina this year, \(R = 9.1\text{.}\)
  4. A 100-meter asteroid will collide with Earth this year, \(R = 8.0\text{.}\)
  5. You will be involved in an automobile accident during a 10-mile trip, \(R = 5.9\text{.}\)
  6. A comet will collide with Earth this year, \(R = 3.5\text{.}\)
  7. You will die in an automobile accident on a 1000-mile trip, \(R=2.3\)
  8. You will die in a plane crash on a 1000-mile trip, \(R = 0.9\text{.}\)
