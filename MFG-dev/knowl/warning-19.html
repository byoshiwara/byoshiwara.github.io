Caution 5.26

A function may have an inverse function even if we cannot find its formula. The function \(f (x) = x^5 + x + 1\) shown in Figure 5.8a is one-to-one, so it has an inverse function. We can even graph the inverse function, as shown in FigureÂ 5.27b, by interchanging the coordinates of points on the graph of \(f\text{.}\) However, we cannot find a formula for the inverse function because we cannot solve the equation \(y = x^5 + x + 1\) for \(x\) in terms of \(y\text{.}\)
