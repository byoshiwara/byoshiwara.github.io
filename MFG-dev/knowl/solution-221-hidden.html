The denominator, \(x+1\text{,}\) equals zero when \(x = -1\text{.}\) Because the numerator does not equal zero when \(x = -1\text{,}\) there is a vertical asymptote at \(x = -1\text{.}\) The asymptote separates the graph into two pieces.

We can use the Table feature of a calculator to evaluate \(g(x)\) for several values of \(x\) on either side of the asymptote, as shown in Figure 7.85a. We plot the points found in this way; then connect the points on either side of the asymptote to obtain the graph shown in Figure 7.85b.
