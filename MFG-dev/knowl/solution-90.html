We raise both sides of the equation to the reciprocal power, \(\dfrac{4}{3}\text{.}\) \begin{align*} \left[(2x + 1)^{3/4}\right]^{4/3} \amp= 27^{4/3} \amp\amp \text{Apply the third law of exponents.} \\ 2x + 1 \amp = 81 \amp\amp \text{Solve as usual.} \\ x \amp = 40 \end{align*}
