Long-Term Behavior of Polynomial Functions.

  1. A polynomial of odd degree (with positive lead coefficient) has negative \(y\)-values for large negative \(x\) and positive \(y\)-values for large positive \(x\text{.}\)
  2. A polynomial of even degree (with positive lead coefficient) has positive \(y\)-values for both large positive and large negative \(x\text{.}\)
