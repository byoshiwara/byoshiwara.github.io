Cubic Bézier Curves: Drawing a Letter y
A cubic Bézier curve is defined by two endpoints, \((x_1, y_1)\) and \((x_4, y_4)\text{,}\) and two control points, \((x_2, y_2)\) and \((x_3, y_3)\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] x \amp= f(t) = x_1\cdot (1 - t)^2 +3x_2\cdot t(1 - t)^2 +3x_3\cdot t^2(1 - t) + x_4\cdot t^3\\ y \amp = g(t) = y_1\cdot (1 - t)^2 +3y_2\cdot t(1 - t)^2 +3y_3\cdot t^2(1 - t) + y_4 \cdot t^3 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
for \(0\le t \le 1\text{.}\)
