Exercise 19.

Nevada was the fastest growing state in the nation between \(1990\) and \(2000\text{,}\) with an annual growth rate of over \(5.2\%\text{.}\)

  1. Write a function for the population of Nevada as a function of time. Let the initial population be \(P_0\text{.}\)

  2. How long will it take for the population to double?

  3. In \(1990\text{,}\) the population of Nevada was \(12\) hundred thousand. Graph your function in the window \(\text{Xmin} = 0\text{,}\) \(\text{Xmax} = 47\text{,}\) \(\text{Ymin} = 0\text{,}\) \(\text{Ymax} = 100\text{.}\)

  4. Use intersect to verify that the population doubles from \(12\) to \(24\text{,}\) from \(24\) to \(48\text{,}\) and from \(48\) to \(96\) hundred thousand people in equal periods of time.
