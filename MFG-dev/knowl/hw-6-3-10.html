Exercise 10.

The annual increase, \(I\text{,}\) in the deer population in a national park depends on the size, \(x\text{,}\) of the population that year, according to the function
\begin{equation*} I = f(x) = 1.2x - 0.0002x^2 \end{equation*}
  1. Find the vertex of the graph. What does it tell us about the deer population?
  2. Sketch the graph for \(0\le x\le 7000\text{.}\)
  3. For what values of \(x\) does the deer population decrease rather than increase? Suggest a reason why the population might decrease.
