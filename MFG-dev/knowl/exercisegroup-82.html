Exercise Group 27–30.

In Problems 27–30, use the graph to estimate the solution to the equation or inequality. Show the solution or solutions on the graph. Then check your answers algebraically.
27.
The figure shows a graph of \(f (x) = \sqrt{x} - 2\text{,}\) for \(x\gt 0\text{.}\) Solve the following:
  1. \(\displaystyle \sqrt{x} - 2=1.5\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \sqrt{x} - 2=2.25\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \sqrt{x} - 2\lt 1\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \sqrt{x} - 2\gt -0.25\)
28.
The figure shows a graph of \(g(x) = \dfrac{4}{x+2} \text{,}\) for \(x\gt -2\text{.}\) Solve the following:
  1. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{4}{x+2}=4\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{4}{x+2}=0.8\)
  3. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{4}{x+2}\gt 1\)
  4. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{4}{x+2}\lt 3\)
29.
The figure shows a graph of \(w(t) = -10(t+1)^3+10 \text{.}\) Solve the following:
  1. \(\displaystyle -10(t+1)^3+10=100\)
  2. \(\displaystyle -10(t+1)^3+10=-140\)
  3. \(\displaystyle -10(t+1)^3+10\gt -50\)
  4. \(\displaystyle -20\lt -10(t+1)^3+10\lt 40\)
30.
The figure shows a graph of \(H(z) =4\sqrt[3]{z-4}+6 \text{.}\) Solve the following:
  1. \(\displaystyle 4\sqrt[3]{z-4}+6=2\)
  2. \(\displaystyle 4\sqrt[3]{z-4}+6=12\)
  3. \(\displaystyle 4\sqrt[3]{z-4}+6\gt 14\)
  4. \(\displaystyle 4\sqrt[3]{z-4}+6\lt 6\)
