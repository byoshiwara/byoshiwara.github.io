Due to improvements in technology, the annual electricity cost of running most major appliances has decreased steadily since \(1970\text{.}\) The average annual cost of running a refrigerator is given, in dollars, by the function

\begin{equation*} C(t) = 148- 28 t^{1/3} \end{equation*}

where \(t\) is the number of years since \(1970\text{.}\)

  1. How much did it cost to run a refrigerator in \(1980\text{?}\) In \(1990\text{?}\)

  2. When was the cost of running a refrigerator half of the cost in \(1970\text{?}\) If the cost continues to decline according to the given function, when will it cost $\(50\) per year to run a refrigerator?

  3. Graph the function \(C(t)\text{.}\) Do you think that the cost will continue to decline indefinitely according to the given function? Why or why not?
