The maximum height that the water stream from a fire hose can reach depends on the water pressure and the diameter of the nozzle, and is given by the function

\begin{equation*} H = f (P, n) = 26 + \dfrac{5}{8}P + 5n \end{equation*}

where \(P\) is the nozzle pressure in psi, and \(n\) is measured in \(18\)-inch increments over the standard nozzle diameter of \(\dfrac{3}{4}\) inch.

  1. Evaluate \(f (40, 2)\) and explain what it means.

  2. What nozzle pressure is needed to reach a height of \(91\) feet with a \(1\dfrac{1}{8}\)-inch nozzle?
