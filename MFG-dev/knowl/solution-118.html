Solution 4.60.1

First, we divide both sides by \(C\) to isolate the power.

\begin{equation*} b^{kt} = 2 \end{equation*}

Next, we take the log base \(10\) of both sides.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] \log b^{kt} \amp = \log 2 \\ kt \log b \amp = \log 2 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Apply Log Property (3).}} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

Finally, we divide both sides by \(k \log b\) to solve for \(t\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} t = \frac{\log 2}{k \log b} \end{equation*}
