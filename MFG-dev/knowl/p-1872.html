The ratio we computed in Example 1.102,

\begin{equation*} \frac{\text{change in elevation}}{\text{change in horizontal position}} \end{equation*}

appears on the graphs as

\begin{equation*} \frac{\text{change in }y\text{-coordinate}}{\text{change in }x\text{-coordinate}} \end{equation*}

For example, as we travel along the line representing Stony Point trail, we move from the point \((0, 0)\) to the point \((2500, 400)\text{.}\) The \(y\)-coordinate changes by \(400\) and the \(x\)-coordinate changes by \(2500\text{,}\) giving the ratio \(0.16\) that we found in Example 1.102. We call this ratio the slope of the line.
