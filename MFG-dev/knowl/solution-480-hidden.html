We use the substitution \(u = e^x\text{,}\) because \(u^2 = \left(e^x\right)^2 = e^{2x}\text{.}\) The original equation then becomes
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] u^2 - 7u + 12 \amp = 0 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Factor the left side.}}\\ (u - 3)(u - 4) \amp = 0 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Apply the zero-factor principle.}}\\ u - 3 \amp = 0 ~~\text{ or } ~~ u - 4 = 0 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Solve each equation for} u.}\\ u \amp =3 ~~\text{ or } ~~ u = 4 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Finally, we replace \(u\) by \(e^x\) and solve for \(x\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] e^x \amp =3 ~~~~~\text{ or }\amp e^x \amp= 4\\ x \amp = \ln 3 ~~~\text{ or }\amp x \amp= \ln 4 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
You should verify that the solutions of the original equation are \(\ln 3\) and \(\ln 4\text{.}\)
