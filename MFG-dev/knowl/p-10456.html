  1. Suppose you invest \(\$1000\) at \(12\%\) annual interest for \(5\) years. In this problem, we will investigate how the number of compounding periods,\(n\text{,}\) affects the amount, \(A\text{.}\) Write \(A\) as a function of \(n\text{,}\) with \(P = 1000\text{,}\) \(r = 0.12\text{,}\) and \(t = 5\text{.}\)
  2. Use your calculator to make a table of values for \(A\) as a function of \(n\text{.}\) What happens to \(A\) as \(n\) increases?
  3. What value of \(n\) is necessary to produce an amount \(A\gt 1818\text{?}\) To produce \(A\gt 1820\text{?}\) To produce \(A\gt 1822\text{?}\)
  4. Graph the function \(A(n)\) in the window
    \begin{align*} {\text{Xmin}} \amp = 0 \amp\amp {\text{Xmax}} = 52\\ {\text{Ymin}} \amp = 1750 \amp\amp {\text{Ymax}} = 1850 \end{align*}
    Describe the graph: Is it increasing or decreasing? Concave up or down? Does it appear to have an asymptote? Give your best estimate for the asymptote.
