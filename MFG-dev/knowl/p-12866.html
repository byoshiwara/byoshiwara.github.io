  • A polynomial of degree \(0\) is a constant, and its graph is a horizontal line. An example of such a polynomial function is \(f(x) = 3\) (see Figure 7.22a).
  • A polynomial of degree \(1\) is a linear function, and its graph is a straight line. The function \(f(x) = 2x - 3\) is an example of a polynomial of degree \(1\text{.}\) (See Figure 7.22b.)
