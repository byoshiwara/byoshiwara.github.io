Use the following formula to answer Problems 43 and 44. If an object is thrown into the air from a height \(s_0\) above the ground with an initial velocity \(v_0\text{,}\) its height \(t\) seconds later is given by the formula

\begin{equation*} h=-\frac{1}{2}gt^2+v_0 t + s_0 \end{equation*}

where \(g\) is a constant that measures the force of gravity.

43.

A tennis ball is thrown into the air with an initial velocity of \(16\) feet per second from a height of \(8\) feet. The value of \(g\) is \(32\text{.}\)

  1. Write a quadratic function that gives the height of the tennis ball at time \(t\text{.}\)

  2. Find the height of the tennis ball at \(t = \dfrac{1}{2}\) second and at \(t = 1\) second.

  3. Write and solve an equation to answer the question: At what time is the tennis ball \(11\) feet high?

  4. Use the Table feature on your calculator to verify your answers to parts (b) and (c). (What value of \(\Delta \text{Tbl}\) is useful for this problem?)

  5. Graph your function from part (a) on your calculator. Use your table to help you choose an appropriate window.

  6. If nobody hits the tennis ball, approximately how long will it be in the air?

44.

A mountain climber stands on a ledge \(80\) feet above the ground and tosses a rope down to a companion clinging to the rock face below the ledge. The initial velocity of the rope is \(-8\) feet per second, and the value of \(g\) is \(32\text{.}\)

  1. Write a quadratic function that gives the height of the rope at time \(t\text{.}\)

  2. What is the height of the rope after \(\dfrac{1}{2} \) second? After \(1\) second?

  3. Write and solve an equation to answer the question: How long does it take the rope to reach the second climber, who is \(17\) feet above the ground?

  4. Use the Table feature on your calculator to verify your answers to parts (b) and (c). (What value of \(\Delta \text{Tbl}\) is useful for this problem?)

  5. Graph your function from part (a) on your calculator. Use your table to help you choose an appropriate window.

  6. If the second climber misses the rope, approximately how long will the rope take to reach the ground?
