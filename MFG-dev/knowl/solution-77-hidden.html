If \(S\) stands for the surface area of a sphere of radius \(r\text{,}\) then

\begin{equation*} S = f (r ) = kr^2 \end{equation*}

To find the constant of variation, \(k\text{,}\) we substitute the values of \(S\) and \(r\text{.}\) \begin{align*} 100\pi \amp = k(5)^2\\ 4\pi \amp= k \end{align*} Thus, \(S = f (r ) = 4\pi r^2\text{.}\)
