First, find the \(x\)-intercepts of the graph by setting \(y\) equal to zero and solving for \(x\text{.}\)
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] 0 \amp= ax^2 + bx\amp\amp \blert{\text{Factor.}} \\ \amp= x(ax + b) \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
Thus,
\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] x \amp=0 \amp\text{or}\amp \amp ax + b \amp= 0\\ x \amp=0 \amp\text{or}\amp \amp x \amp =\frac{-b}{a} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
The \(x\)-intercepts are the points \((0, 0)\) and \((\dfrac{-b}{a}, 0)\text{.}\)
