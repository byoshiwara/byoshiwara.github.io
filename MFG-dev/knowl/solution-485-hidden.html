1. First, write the equation with the constant term on the right side.

    \begin{equation*} x^2 - 4x{} ~ \fillinmath{XXXXXX} {}=3 \end{equation*}

  2. Now complete the square on the left side. The coefficient of \(x\) is \(-4\text{,}\) so

    \begin{equation*} p = \frac{1}{2}(-4) = -2 ~~~\text{ and } ~~~ p^2 = (-2)^2 = 4 \end{equation*}
    We add \(4\) to both sides of our equation:
    \begin{equation*} x^2 - 4x \alert{{}+4}=3\alert{{}+4} \end{equation*}

  3. Write the left side as the square of a binomial, and combine terms on the right side:

    \begin{equation*} (x - 2)^2 =7 \end{equation*}

  4. Finally, use extraction of roots to obtain

    \begin{align*} x - 2 \amp =\sqrt{7} \amp\text{or} \amp\amp x - 2 \amp= -\sqrt{7} \amp \blert{\text{Solve each equation.}}\\ x \amp =2+\sqrt{7} \amp\text{or} \amp\amp x \amp=2 -\sqrt{7} \end{align*}
