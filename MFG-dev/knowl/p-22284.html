  1. A sequence in which each term can be obtained from the previous term by adding a fixed amount is called an arithmetic sequence.
  2. The fixed amount between two successive terms of an arithmetic sequence is called the common difference.
  3. An arithmetic sequence defines a linear function of \(n\text{.}\)
  4. The \(n^{\text{th}}\) term of an arithmetic sequence is \(a_n = a + (n-1)d\text{.}\)
  5. A sequence in which each term can be obtained from the previous term by multiplying by a fixed amount is called a geometric sequence.
  6. The ratio of two successive terms of a geometridc sequence is called the common ratio.
  7. A geometric sequence defines an exponential function of \(n\text{.}\)
  8. The \(n^{\text{th}}\) term of a geometric sequence is \(a_n = ar^{n-1}\text{.}\)
