Problems 53 and 54 prove that the vertical line \(x =\dfrac{-b}{2a}\) is the axis of symmetry of the graph of \(y = ax^2 + bx + c\text{.}\) A graph is symmetric about the line \(x = h\) if the point \((h + d, v)\) lies on the graph whenever the point \((h - d, v)\) lies on the graph.

53

  1. Sketch a parabola \(y = a(x - h)^2 + k\) and the line \(x = h\text{.}\) We will show that the parabola is symmetric about the line \(x = h\text{.}\)

  2. Label a point on the parabola with \(x\)-coordinate \(x = h + d\text{,}\) where \(d\gt 0\text{.}\) What is the \(y\)-coordinate of that point?

  3. Label the point on the parabola with \(x\)-coordinate \(x = h - d\text{.}\) What is the \(y\)-coordinate of that point?

  4. Explain why your answers to parts (b) and (c) prove that the line \(x = h\) is the axis of symmetry for the graph of \(y = a(x - h)^2 + k\text{.}\)
