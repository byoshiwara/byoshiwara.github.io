The effective half-life, denoted by \(T_e\text{,}\) is related to the biological and physical half-lives by the equation

\begin{equation*} \frac{1}{T_e}=\frac{1}{T_b}+\frac{1}{T_p} \end{equation*}

The radioisotope \(^{131}\text{I}\) is used as a label for the human serum albumin. The physical half-life of \(^{131}\text{I}\) is \(8\) days. (Source: Pope, 1989)

  1. If \(^{131}\text{I}\) is cleared from the body with a half-life of \(21\) days, what is the effective half-life of \(^{131}\text{I}\text{?}\)

  2. The biological half-life of a substance varies considerably from person to person. If the biological half-life of \(^{131}\text{I}\) is \(x\) days, what is the effective half life?

  3. Let \(f (x)\) represent the effective half-life of \(^{131}\text{I}\) when the biological half-life is \(x\) days. Graph \(y = f (x)\text{.}\)

  4. What would the biological half-life of \(^{131}\text{I}\) need to be to produce an effective half-life of \(6\) days? Label the corresponding point on your graph.

  5. For what possible biological half-lives of \(^{131}\text{I}\) will the effective half-life be less than \(4\) days?
