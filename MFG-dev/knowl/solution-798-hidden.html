We first collect all the variable terms on one side of the equation, and the constant terms on the other side.
\begin{align*} 3x - 5 \alert{{}- x} \amp = x + 3 \alert{{}- x}\amp\amp \blert{\text{Subtract }x \text{ from both sides.}}\\ 2x - 5 \amp = 3\amp\amp \blert{\text{Simplify.}}\\ 2x - 5 \alert{{}+ 5} \amp = 5 \alert{{}+ 5}\amp\amp \blert{\text{Add 5 to both sides.}}\\ 2x \amp = 8 \amp\amp\blert{\text{Simplify.}}\\ \frac{2x}{\alert{2}}\amp =\frac{8}{\alert{2}}\amp\amp\blert{\text{Divide both sides by 2.}}\\ x\amp = 4\amp\amp\blert{\text{Simplify.}} \end{align*}
The solution is \(4\text{.}\) (You can check the solution by substituting \(4\) into the original equation to show that a true statement results.)
