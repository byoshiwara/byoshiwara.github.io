Use the point-slope form to find the equation of the line that passes through the point \((-3, 5)\) and has slope \(-1.4\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] y \amp= y_1 + m(x - x_1) \amp\amp\blert{{\small\text{Substitute }-1.4\text{ for }m\text{ and }(-3, 5)\text{ for }(x_1, y_1).}}\\ \amp\amp\amp\blert{{\small\text{Simplify: Apply the distributive law.}}} \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
