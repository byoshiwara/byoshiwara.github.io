Let \(I\) stand for the intensity of the signal in watts per square meter, and \(d\) for the distance from the station in kilometers. Then \(I = \dfrac{k}{d^2}\text{.}\) To find the constant \(k\text{,}\) we substitute \(0.016\) for \(I\) and \(1\) for \(d\text{.}\) Solving for \(k\) gives us \begin{align*} 0.016 \amp = \dfrac{k}{1^2} \\ k \amp = 0.016 (1^2) = 0.016 \end{align*} Thus, \(I = \dfrac{0.016}{d^2}\text{.}\)
