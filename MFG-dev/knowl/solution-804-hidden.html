1. \([3, 6)\text{.}\) This is called a half-open or half-closed interval.

  2. \([-9,\infty)\text{.}\) We always use round brackets next to the symbol \(\infty\) because \(\infty\) is not a specific number and is not included in the set.

  3. \((-\infty, 1] \cup (4, \infty)\text{.}\) The word or describes the union of two sets.

  4. \((-8,-5] \cup [-1, 3)\text{.}\)
