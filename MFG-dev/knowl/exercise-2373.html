Exercise 43.
A tennis ball is thrown into the air with an initial velocity of \(16\) feet per second from a height of \(8\) feet. The value of \(g\) is \(32\text{.}\)
  1. Write a quadratic function that gives the height of the tennis ball at time \(t\text{.}\)
  2. Find the height of the tennis ball at \(t = \dfrac{1}{2}\) second and at \(t = 1\) second.
  3. Write and solve an equation to answer the question: At what time is the tennis ball \(11\) feet high?
  4. Use the Table feature on your calculator to verify your answers to parts (b) and (c). (What value of \(\Delta \text{Tbl}\) is useful for this problem?)
  5. Graph your function from part (a) on your calculator. Use your table to help you choose an appropriate window.
  6. If nobody hits the tennis ball, approximately how long will it be in the air?
