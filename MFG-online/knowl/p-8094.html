The value of \(t\) that solves the equation is called the base \(2\) logarithm of \(16\text{.}\) Since \(2^4 = 16\text{,}\) the base \(2\) logarithm of \(16\) is \(4\text{.}\) We write this as

\begin{equation*} \log_{2}16 = 4 \end{equation*}

In other words, we solve an exponential equation by computing a logarithm. You can check that \(t = \alert{4}\) solves the problem stated above:
