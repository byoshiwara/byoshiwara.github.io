Remark6.82Solving an Inequality With a Graphing Calculator

You can use your graphing calculator to solve the problem in Example 6.80. Graph the two equations \begin{align} Y_1 \amp= 256X − 16X^2\\ Y_2 \amp= 800 \end{align} on the same screen. Use WINDOW settings to match the graph in Figure 6.83. Then use the intersect feature to find the \(x\)-coordinates of the points where the two graphs intersect (there are two of them). These points will have \(y\)-coordinates of \(800\). The parabola is above the line, so \(h \gt 800\) when \(t\) is between these two \(x\)-values. To two decimal places, you can see that \(h \gt 800\) when \(4.26 \lt t \lt 11.74\).
