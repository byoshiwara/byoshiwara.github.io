Using the wrong type of equation to fit the data is a common error in making predictions. We know that the minute hand of a clock repeats its position every \(60\) minutes. The graph of the height of its tip oscillates up and down, repeating the same pattern over and over. We cannot describe such a graph using either a linear or a quadratic equation.
