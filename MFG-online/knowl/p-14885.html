We can solve any cubic equation by first using a substitution to put the equation in Tartaglia's special form.

  1. Consider the equation \(X^3 + bX^2 + cX + d = 0\text{.}\) Make the substitution \(X = x - \dfrac{b}{3}\text{,}\) and expand the left side of the equation.

  2. What is the coefficient of \(x^2\) in the resulting equation? What are the values of \(m\) and \(n\text{?}\)

  3. If you solve the special form in part (a) for \(x\text{,}\) how can you find the value of \(X\) that solves the original equation?
