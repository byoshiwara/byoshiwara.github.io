We use the substitution \(u = e^x\text{,}\) because \(u^2 = \left(e^x\right)^2 = e^{2x}\text{.}\) The original equation then becomes \begin{align*} u^2 - 7u + 12 \amp= 0\amp\amp\text{Factor the left side.}\\ (u - 3)(u - 4) \amp = 0\amp\amp\text{Apply the zero-factor principle.}\\ u - 3 \amp =0 ~~\text{ or } ~~ u - 4 = 0\amp\amp\text{Solve each equation for u.}\\ u \amp =3 ~~\text{ or } ~~ u = 4 \end{align*} Finally, we replace \(u\) by \(e^x\) and solve for \(x\text{.}\) \begin{align*} e^x \amp =3 ~~~~~\text{ or }\amp e^x \amp= 4\\ x \amp = \ln 3 ~~~\text{ or }\amp x \amp= \ln 4 \end{align*} You should verify that the solutions of the original equation are \(\ln 3\) and \(\ln 4\text{.}\)
