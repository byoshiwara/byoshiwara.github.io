As we move from left to right on this scale, we multiply the value at the previous tick mark by \(10^{0.1}\text{,}\) or about \(1.258\text{.}\) For example, \begin{align*} 10^{0.2} \amp = 1.258 × 10^{0.1} = 1.585\\ 10^{0.3} \amp = 1.585 × 10^{0.1} = 1.995 \end{align*} and so on. Moving up by equal increments on a log scale does not add equal amounts to the values plotted; it multiplies the values by equal factors.
