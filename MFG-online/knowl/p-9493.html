In Example 5.14, we used a graph of \(h\) to read values of \(h^{-1}\text{.}\) But we can also plot the graph of \(h^{-1}\) itself. Because \(C\) is the input variable for \(h^{-1}\text{,}\) we plot \(C\) on the horizontal axis and \(F\) on the vertical axis. To find some points on the graph of \(h^{-1}\text{,}\) we interchange the coordinates of points on the graph of \(h\text{.}\) The graph of \(h^{-1}\) is shown in Figure 5.17.
