There is a convenient notation for discussing functions. First, we choose a letter, such as \(f\text{,}\) \(g\text{,}\) or \(h\) (or \(F\text{,}\) \(G\text{,}\) or \(H\)), to name a particular function. (We can use any letter, but these are the most common choices.) For instance, in ExampleÂ 1.34, the height, \(h\text{,}\) of a falling algebra book is a function of the elapsed time, \(t\text{.}\) We might call this function \(f\text{.}\) In other words, \(f\) is the name of the relationship between the variables \(h\) and \(t\text{.}\) We write

\begin{equation*} h = f (t) \end{equation*}

which means "\(h\) is a function of \(t\text{,}\) and \(f\) is the name of the function."
