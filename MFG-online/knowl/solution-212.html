Solution7.55.1

We multiply numerator and denominator by \(\alert{4 + 2i}\text{,}\) the conjugate of the denominator.

\begin{align*} \frac{2+3i}{4-2i}\amp=\frac{(2+3i)(\alert{4 + 2i})}{(4-2i)(\alert{4 + 2i})} \amp\amp\text{Expand numerator and denominator.}\\ \amp=\frac{8 + 4i + 12i + 6i^2}{16 + 8i - 8i - 4i^2} \amp\amp\text{Replace }i^2\text{ by } -1.\\ \amp= \frac{8 + 16i - 6}{16 - (-4)} \amp\amp\text{Combine like terms.}\\ \amp= \frac{2 + 16i}{20} \amp\amp\text{Divide 20 into each term of numerator.}\\ \amp= \frac{2}{20}+ \frac{16i}{20}\\ \amp= \frac{1}{10}+ \frac{4}{5}i \end{align*} in-context
