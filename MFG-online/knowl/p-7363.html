  1. If a quantity is multiplied by a constant factor, \(b\text{,}\) in each time period, we say that it undergoes exponential growth or decay. The constant \(b\) is called the growth factor if \(b\gt 1\) and the decay factor if \(0 \lt b \lt 1\text{.}\)

  2. Quantities that increase or decrease by a constant percent in each time period grow or decay exponentially.

  3. Exponential Growth and Decay

    The function

    \begin{equation*} P(t) = P_0 b^t \end{equation*}

    models exponential growth and decay.

    \(P_0 = P(0) \) is the initial value of \(P\text{;}\)

    \(b\) is the growth or decay factor.

    1. If \(b\gt 1\text{,}\) then \(P(t)\) is increasing, and \(b = 1 + r\text{,}\) where \(r\) represents percent increase.

    2. If \(0\lt b\lt 1\text{,}\) then \(P(t)\) is decreasing, and \(b = 1 - r\text{,}\) where \(r\) represents percent decrease.
