1. Let \(x\) stand for the size of the smallest angle. Then the medium-sized angle must be \(2x-15\text{.}\)

  2. Because the right angle is the largest angle, the sum of the smallest and medium-sized angles must be the remaining \(90\degree\text{.}\) Thus,

    \begin{equation*} x + (2x - 15) = 90 \end{equation*}

  3. Solve the equation. Begin by simplifying the left side.

    \begin{align*} 3x - 15 \amp = 90\amp\amp\text{Add 15 to both sides.} \\ 3x \amp = 105\amp\amp\text{Divide both sides by 3.}\\ x \amp = 35 \end{align*}

  4. The smallest angle is \(35\degree\text{,}\) and the medium-sized angle is \(2(35\degree) -15\degree\text{,}\) or \(55\degree\text{.}\)
