The area of an equilateral triangle is given by the formula \(A = \dfrac{d\sqrt{3}}{4}s^2\text{,}\) where \(s\) is the length of the side.

  1. Find the areas of equilateral triangles with sides of length \(2\) centimeters, \(4\) centimeters, and \(10\) centimeters. First give exact values, then approximations to hundredths.

  2. Graph the area equation in the window

    \begin{align*} \small{\text{Xmin}} \amp = 0 \amp\amp \small{\text{Xmax}} = 14.1\\ \small{\text{Ymin}} \amp = 0 \amp\amp \small{\text{Ymax}} = 60 \end{align*}

    Use the TRACE or value feature to verify your answers to part (a).

  3. Trace along the curve to the point \((5.1, 11.26266)\text{.}\) What do the coordinates of this point represent?

  4. Use your graph to estimate the side of an equilateral triangle whose area is \(20\) square centimeters.

  5. Write and solve an equation to answer part (d).

  6. If the area of an equilateral triangle is \(100\sqrt{3}\) square centimeters, what is the length of its side?
