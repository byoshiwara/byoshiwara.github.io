Solution4.85.1

We want to find a function \(C(t) = ab^t\) for the number of cedi to the dollar, where \(t = 0\) in 1986. We have two function values, \(C(7) = 720\text{,}\) and \(C(10) = 1620\text{,}\) and with these values we can write two equations. \begin{align*} ab^7 \amp = 720 \\ ab^{10} \amp = 1620 \end{align*} We divide the second equation by the first to find \begin{align*} \frac{ab^{10}}{ab^7} \amp= \frac{1620}{720} \\ b^3 \amp= 2.25 \end{align*} Now we can solve this last equation for \(b\) to get \(b = \sqrt[3]{2.25}\approx 1.31\text{.}\) Finally, we substitute \(b = 1.31\) into the first equation to find \(a\text{.}\) \begin{align*} a(1.31)^7 \amp = 720 \\ a \amp = \frac{720}{1.317} \\ \amp = 108.75 \end{align*} Thus, \(C(t) = 108.75(1.31)^t\) , and the annual inflation rate was \(31\)%.
