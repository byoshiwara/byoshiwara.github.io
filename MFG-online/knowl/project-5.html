Project1.7Duration of eclipse

The time, \(T\text{,}\) it takes for the Moon to eclipse the Sun totally is given (in minutes) by the formula

\begin{equation*} T=\frac{1}{v}\left(\frac{rD}{R}-d \right) \end{equation*}

where \(d\) is the diameter of the Moon, \(D\) is the diameter of the Sun, \(r\) is the distance from the Earth to the Moon, \(R\) is the distance from the Earth to the Sun, and \(v\) is the speed of the Moon.

  1. Solve the formula for \(v\) in terms of the other variables.

  2. It takes \(2.68\) minutes for the Moon to eclipse the Sun. Calculate the speed of the Moon given the following values:

    \begin{align*} d = 3.48 × 103 \text{ km}\amp\amp D = 1.41 × 106 \text{ km}\\ r = 3.82 × 105 \text{ km} \amp\amp R = 1.48 × 108 \text{ km} \end{align*}
