Solution8.64.1

We solve the inequality for \(y\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} 4x - 3y \amp\ge 12\\ -3y\amp\ge -4x + 12\\ y\amp\le \frac{4}{3}x-4 \end{align*}

We graph the corresponding line

\begin{equation*} y = \frac{4}{3}x-4 \end{equation*}

Note that the \(y\)-intercept is \(-4\) and the slope is \(\frac{4}{3}\text{.}\) (See Section 1.4 to review the slope-intercept method of graphing.) Finally, we shade the half-plane below the line. The completed graph is shown in Figure 8.65.
