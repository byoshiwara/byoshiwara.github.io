We look for a perfect square that divides evenly into 45. The largest perfect square that divides 45is 9, so we factor 45 as \(9 \cdot 5\) Then we use the product rule to write

\begin{equation*} \sqrt{45}=\sqrt{9 \cdot 5} = \sqrt{9}\sqrt{5} \end{equation*}

Finally, we simplify \(\sqrt{9}\) to get

\begin{equation*} \sqrt{45}= \sqrt{9}\sqrt{5} =3\sqrt{5} \end{equation*}
