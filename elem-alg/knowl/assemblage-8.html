Look Closer

In arithmetic we use the symbol \(\times\) to denote multiplication. However, in algebra, \(\times\) may be confused with the variable \(x\text{.}\) So, instead, we use a dot or parentheses for multiplication, like this:

If one of the factors is a variable, we can just write the factors side by side. For example, \begin{align*} ab~~~~\amp \text{means} \amp\amp \text{the product of}~~ a~~ \text{times}~~ b\\ 3x~~~~\amp \text{means} \amp\amp \text{the product of}~~ 3~~ \text{times}~~ x\\ \end{align*}
