Activity 2.12. Equivalent Expressions.

    1. Show that the expressions \(6+2x\) and \(8x\) are equal if \(x=1\text{:}\)

      \begin{align*} 6+2x \amp = \underline{\hspace{3.636363636363636em}}\\ 8x \amp = \underline{\hspace{3.636363636363636em}} \end{align*}

    2. Show that the expressions \(6+2x\) and \(8x\) are not equal if \(x=2\text{:}\)

      \begin{align*} 6+2x \amp = \underline{\hspace{3.636363636363636em}}\\ 8x \amp = \underline{\hspace{3.636363636363636em}} \end{align*}
    3. Are the expressions \(6+2x\) and \(8x\) equivalent?

  2. Explain the following phrases, and give an example for each.

    1. equivalent expressions
    2. like terms
    3. numerical coefficient

  3. Combine like terms by adding or subtracting.

    1. \(\displaystyle -2x+6x-x\)
    2. \(\displaystyle 3bc-(-4bc)-8bc\)

  4. Simplify by removing parentheses and combining like terms.

    1. \(\displaystyle -5u-6uv+(8uv+9u)\)
    2. \(\displaystyle (32-26)+ (-3+2h)\)
    3. \(\displaystyle (3a-2)-2a-(5-2a)\)
