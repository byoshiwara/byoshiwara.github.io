Example: Solve \(~~5(z-6) = 65\)

  1. Analyze the expression \(~4x-5\text{.}\)

    \begin{align*} \amp \blert{\text{Operations performed on}~x} \amp \amp \blert{\text{Steps for solution}}\\ \amp 1.~~~ \text{Subtract 6} \amp \amp 1.~~~ \text{Divide by 5}\\ \amp 2.~~~ \text{Multiply by 5} \amp \amp 2.~~~\text{Add 6} \end{align*}

  2. Carry out the solution.

    \begin{align*} 5(z-6) \amp = 65 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by 5.}}\\ \dfrac{5(x-6)}{\alert{5}} \amp = \dfrac{65}{\alert{5}}\\ z-6 \amp = 13 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Add 6 to both sides.}}\\ z-6 \alert{+6} \amp = 13 \alert{+6}\\ z \amp = 19 \end{align*}

    The solutions is \(19\text{.}\) \(~~~~~~~~\blert{\text{Check:}}~~~~5(\alert{19}-6) = 65\)
