Look Closer.

To improve the accuracy of the graph in Example 2, we can find a third point on the line by writing the slope in an equivalent form. We change the sign of both numerator and denominator of the slope to get

\begin{equation*} m = \dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{-3}{-4} \end{equation*}

Starting again from the \(y\)-intercept \((0,-2)\text{,}\) we now move 3 units down and 4 units left, and find the point \((-4,-5)\) on the graph.
