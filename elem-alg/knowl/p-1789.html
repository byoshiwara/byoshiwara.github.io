We can verify that the two equations in Example 5 are actually equivalent by solving the second equation for \(y\) in terms of \(x\text{.}\)

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} 6x-3y \amp = -6 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Add}~3y~\text{and 6 to both sides.}} \\ 6x+6 \amp = 3y \amp\amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by 3.}}\\ 2x+2 \amp = y \end{aligned} \end{equation*}

The second equation is the same as the first equation. A system in which the two equations are equivalent is called dependent. A dependent system has infinitely many solutions: Every point on the graph is a solution to the system.
