Remember that we do not obtain an equivalent equation if we multiply both sides of an equation by zero. In Example 3 we multiplied by \(4(x-5)\text{.}\) Is it possible that \(4(x-5)\) equals zero? After solving, we found that \(x=9\text{,}\) so \(4(x-5) = 16\text{,}\) which is not zero, and the multiplication step was valid.
