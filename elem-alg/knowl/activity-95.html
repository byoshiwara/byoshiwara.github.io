Activity 8.3. Multiplying and Dividing Fractions.

For Problems 1-5, review the Example, then complete the Exercise.

  1. Example: \(~~~\dfrac{5}{8} \cdot \dfrac{3}{4} = \dfrac{5 \cdot 3}{8 \cdot 4} = \dfrac{15}{32}~~\)

    \(\blert{\text{Multiply the numerators together; multiply the denominators together.}}\)

    1. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{2}{5} \cdot \dfrac{2}{3} =\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{2}{w} \cdot \dfrac{z}{3} =\)

  2. Example: \(~~~\dfrac{3}{4} \cdot \dfrac{5}{6} = \dfrac{\cancel{3}}{4} \cdot \dfrac{5}{2 \cdot \cancel{3}} = \dfrac{5}{8}~~\)

    \(\blert{\text{Divide out common factors first, then multiply.}}\)

    1. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{5}{4} \cdot \dfrac{8}{9} =\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{5}{2a} \cdot \dfrac{4a}{9} =\)

  3. Example: \(~~~\dfrac{3}{4} \cdot 6 = \dfrac{\cancel{3}}{4} \cdot \dfrac{6}{1} = \dfrac{3}{2 \cdot \cancel{2}} \cdot \dfrac{\cancel{2}\cdot 3}{1} = \dfrac{9}{2}~~~~~~~~ \blert{\text{Write} ~6~ \text{as} ~\dfrac{6}{1}.}\)

    1. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{7}{3} \cdot 12 =\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{7}{x} \cdot 4x =\)

  4. Example: \(~~~\dfrac{12}{5} \div \dfrac{8}{5} = \dfrac{12}{5} \cdot \dfrac{5}{8} = \dfrac{\cancel{4} \cdot 3}{\cancel{5}} \cdot \dfrac{\cancel{5}}{\cancel{4}\cdot 2} = \dfrac{3}{2}~~\)

    \(\blert{\text{Take the reciprocal of the second fraction; then change to multiplication.}}\)

    1. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{8}{3} \div \dfrac{2}{9} =\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{4a}{3b} \div \dfrac{2a}{3} =\)

  5. Example: \(~~~\dfrac{3}{5} \div 6 = \dfrac{3}{5} \cdot \dfrac{1}{6} = \dfrac{\cancel{3}}{5} \cdot \dfrac{1}{\cancel{3}\cdot 2} = \dfrac{1}{15}~~\)

    1. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{2}{3} \div 4 =\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{2}{3y} \div 4y =\)

  6. Multiply:

    1. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{-5}{b}a = \dfrac{-5}{b} \cdot \dfrac{a}{1} =\)
    2. \(\displaystyle 6x\left(\dfrac{2}{x^2-x}\right)=\dfrac{6x}{1} \cdot \dfrac{2}{x^2-x} =\)
