To understand why the product of two negative numbers is positive, you might think of canceling, or subtracting, several debts of the same amount. Or, you could observe the pattern in the lists of products below. As we move down the list, the product increases by equal amounts.

\begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} 3(-2) \amp =-6 \amp 3(-5) \amp =-15\\ 2(-2) \amp =-4 \amp 2(-5) \amp =-10\\ 1(-2) \amp =-2 \amp 1(-5) \amp =-5~~\amp \leftarrow \text{Positive times negative is negative.}\\ 0(-2) \amp =-0 \amp 0(-5) \amp =0\\ -1(-2) \amp =2 \amp -1(-5) \amp =5~~\amp \leftarrow \text{Negative times negative is positive.}\\ -2(-2) \amp =4 \amp -2(-5) \amp =10\\ -3(-2) \amp =6 \amp -3(-5) \amp =15 \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
