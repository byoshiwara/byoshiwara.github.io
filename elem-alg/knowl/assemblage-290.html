Look Closer.

Suppose we'd like to evaluate the fraction in Example 6 for \(x=478\text{.}\) Instead of substituting 478 into the original fraction, it is much simpler to use the reduced fraction, and get

\begin{equation*} \dfrac{x}{2}=\dfrac{\alert{478}}{2} = 239 \end{equation*}

This the whole point of reducing a fraction: to get a simpler but equivalent expression.
