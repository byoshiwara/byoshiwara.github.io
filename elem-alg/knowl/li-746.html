Item b.
We see that \(y\) has been replaced by \(-2\) in the equation of the graph. So we look for the point on the graph that has \(y\)-coordinate \(-2\text{.}\) This point, labeled \(P\) on the graph below, has \(x\)-coordinate \(-3\text{.}\)
Because it lies on the graph, the point \(P(-3,-2)\) is a solution of the equation \(~y=\dfrac{-2}{3}x-4\text{.}\) When we substitute \(x=-3\) and \(y=-2\) into the equation, we get a true statement:
\begin{equation*} -2=\dfrac{-2}{3}(-3)-4 \end{equation*}
But this statement also tells us that \(-3\) is a solution of the equation \(~\dfrac{-2}{3}x-4=-2\text{.}\)
