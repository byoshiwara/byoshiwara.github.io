Numbers greater than zero are called positive numbers, and numbers less than zero are negative numbers. We use a number line to illustrate relationships among numbers.

To the right of zero we mark the numbers 1, 2, 3, 4, ... at evenly spaced intervals. These numbers are called the natural or counting numbers. The whole numbers include the natural numbers and zero: 0, 1, 2, 3, ... .
