Exercise 2.

  1. Graph the line of slope 3 that passes through the point \((-4,-6)\text{.}\)

    1. \(\displaystyle \blert{\text{Plot the point}~~(-4,-6).}\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \blert{\text{Use the slope to find a second point:} ~~~m =\dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{3}{1}}\)

    3. \(\displaystyle \blert{\text{Use the slope to find a third point:} ~~~m =\dfrac{\Delta y}{\Delta x} = \dfrac{-3}{-1}}\)

  2. Find an equation for the line.

    1. \(\displaystyle \blert{\text{Use the point-slope formula}~\dfrac{y-y_1}{x-x_1} = m.}\)
    2. \(\displaystyle \blert{\text{Cross-multiply to simplify the equation.}}\)
    3. \(\displaystyle \blert{\text{Solve for} ~y.} \)
