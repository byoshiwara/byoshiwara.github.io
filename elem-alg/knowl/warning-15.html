Caution 2.39.

We must be careful when removing parentheses preceded by a minus sign. In the expression

\begin{equation*} (5x-2)-(7x-4) \end{equation*}

the minus sign preceding \((7x-4)\) applies to each term inside the parentheses. Therefore, when we remove the parentheses we must change the sign of \(7x\) from \(+\) to \(-\) and we must change the sign of \(-4\) from \(-\) to \(+\text{.}\) Thus,

\begin{align*} (5x-2)-(7x-4)\amp = 5x-2\alert{-}7x\alert{+}4 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Change each sign inside parentheses.}}\\ \amp =5x-7x-2+4 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Combine like terms.}}\\ \amp =-2x+2 \end{align*}
