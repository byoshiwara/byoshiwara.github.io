Start with an inequality that we know is true, say \(\blert{3 \lt 5}\text{.}\) Which of the following operations keeps the inequality true?

  1. Add a positive number, say 4, to both sides of the inequality, to get

    \begin{align*} 3 \blert{+4} \amp \lt 5 \blert{+4}\\ 7 \amp \lt 9 \end{align*}

  2. Subtract the same quantity, say 6, from both sides of the inequality, to get

    \begin{align*} 3 \blert{-6} \amp \lt 5 \blert{-6}\\ -3 \amp \lt -1 \end{align*}

  3. Multiply both sides of the inequality by a positive number, say 2, to get

    \begin{align*} \blert{2}(3) \amp \lt \blert{2}(5)\\ 6 \amp \lt 10 \end{align*}

  4. Divide both sides of the inequality by a positive number, say 4, to get

    \begin{align*} \dfrac{3}{\blert{4}} \amp \lt \dfrac{5}{\blert{4}} \end{align*}

  5. Multiply both sides of the inequality by a negative number, say \(-2\text{,}\) to get

    \begin{align*} \blert{-2}(3) \amp \lt \blert{-2}(5)\\ -6 \amp \lt -10~~~~~~~~\blert{\text{False!}} \end{align*}
