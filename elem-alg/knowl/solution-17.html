Solution 1.22.1.

  1. We locate \(D=7\) on the horizontal axis, as shown at left below. Then we move vertically to point on the graph with \(D\)-coordinate 7. Finally, we move horizontally from point \(A\) to the vertical axis to find the \(F\)-coordinate. The coordinates of point \(A\) are \((7,11)\text{,}\) which tells us that when \(D=7,~F=11\text{.}\) Our answer is 11.

    To verify the answer algebraically, we evaluate the expression for \(D=7\text{:}\) we substitute \(\alert{7}\) for \(D\) into the equation:

    \begin{equation*} F=D+4=\alert{7}+4=11 \end{equation*}

  2. We locate \(F=13\) on the vertical axis. We move horizontally to point \(B\) on the graph shown above right, with \(F\)-coordinate 13. From point \(B\text{,}\) we move vertically to the horizontal axis to find the \(D\)-coordinate. The coordinates of point \(B\) are \((9,13)\text{,}\) which tells us that when \(F=13,~D=9\text{.}\) Our answer is 9.

    To verify the answer algebraically, we solve the equation when \(F=13\text{:}\) we subtract 4 from both sides of the equation:

    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned} 13 \amp = D+4 \amp\amp \blert{\text{Subtract 4 from both sides.}}\\ \blert{-4} \amp ~~~~~~~~~\blert{-4}\\ 9 \amp = D \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
