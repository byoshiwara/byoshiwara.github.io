To find the \(x\)-intercept of the graph, we substitute \(0\) for \(y\) and solve for \(x\text{.}\)

\begin{align*} 3 + 2(\alert{0}) \amp = 7 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Simplify the left side.}}\\ 3x \amp = 7 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by 3.}}\\ x \amp = \dfrac{7}{3} = 2\dfrac{1}{3} \end{align*}

The \(x\)-intercept is the point \((2\dfrac{1}{3}, 0)\text{.}\)
