Example: Solve \(~~4x-5=7\)

  1. Analyze the expression \(~4x-5\text{.}\)

    \begin{align*} \amp \blert{\text{Operations performed on}~x} \amp \amp \blert{\text{Steps for solution}}\\ \amp 1.~~~ \text{Multiply by 4} \amp \amp 1.~~~ \text{Add 5}\\ \amp 2.~~~ \text{Subtract 5} \amp \amp 2.~~~\text{Divide by 4} \end{align*}

  2. Carry out the solution.

    \begin{align*} 4x-5 \amp = 7 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Add 5 to both sides.}}\\ 4x-5 \alert{+ 5} \amp = 7 \alert{+ 5}\\ 4x \amp = 12 \amp \amp \blert{\text{Divide both sides by 4.}}\\ \dfrac{4x}{\alert{4}} \amp = \dfrac{12}{\alert{4}}\\ x \amp = 3 \end{align*}

    The solutions is 3. \(~~~~~~~~\blert{\text{Check:}}~~~~4(\alert{3}) - 5 =7\)
