Example A.67.

Reduce each fraction.
  1. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{4x+2}{4}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{9x^2+3}{6x+3}\)
Solution.
Factor the numerator and denominator. Then divide numerator and denominator by the common factors.
  1. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{4x + 2}{4} = \dfrac{\Ccancel[blue]{2}(2x + 1)}{\Ccancel[blue]{2} (2)}\dfrac{2x+1}{2}\)
  2. \(\displaystyle \dfrac{9x^2+3}{6x+3} = \dfrac{\Ccancel[blue]{3}(3x^2 + 1)}{\Ccancel[blue]{3} (2x+1)} = \dfrac{3x^2 + 1}{2x+1}\)
