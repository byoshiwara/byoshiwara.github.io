Checkpoint 1.88. Practice 5.

  1. Use the graph of \(y = 30 - 8x\) shown above to solve the equation
    \begin{equation*} 30 - 8x = 50 \end{equation*}
  2. Verify your solution algebraically.
Solution.
  1. The point on the line where the \(y\)-coordinate is \(50\) is the point \((-2.5,50)\text{,}\) so \(x=-2.5\) is the solution.
  2. We verify that we have an identity when we substitute \(x=-2.5\) into the equation \(30 - 8x = 50\text{.}\)
    \begin{align*} 30-8(\alert{-2.5}) \amp \stackrel{\text{?}}{=} 50\\ 30 - (-20) \amp = 50 \amp \blert{\text{Yes!}} \end{align*}
