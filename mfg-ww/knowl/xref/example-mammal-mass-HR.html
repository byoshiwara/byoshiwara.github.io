Example 3.70.

A typical human male weighs about \(70\) kilograms and has a resting heart rate of \(70\) beats per minute.
  1. Find the constant of proportionality, \(k\text{,}\) and write a formula for \(H(m)\text{.}\)
  2. Fill in the table with the heart rates of the mammals whose masses are given.
    Animal Shrew Rabbit Cat Wolf Horse Polar bear Elephant Whale
    Mass (kg) \(0.004\) \(2\) \(4\) \(80\) \(300\) \(600\) \(5400\) \(70,000\)
    Heart rate \(\) \(\) \(\) \(\) \(\) \(\) \(\) \(\)
  3. Sketch a graph of \(H\) for masses up to \(6000\) kilograms.
Solution.
  1. We substitute \(H = 70\) and \(m = 70\) into the equation; then solve for \(k\text{.}\)
    \begin{equation*} \begin{aligned}[t] 70 \amp = k \cdot 70^{-1/4} \\ k \amp= \frac{70}{70^{-1/4}} = 70^{5/4} \approx 202.5 \\ \end{aligned} \end{equation*}
    Thus, \(H(m) = 202.5m^{-1/4}\text{.}\)
  2. We evaluate the function \(H\) for each of the masses given in the table.
